🌸 Phrase 天长地久

Everyday example. Imagine two childhood friends who grow up, move to different cities, start families, and grow old — yet their bond never fades. In English you might say their friendship "stood the test of time." Chinese has a single four-character phrase for that feeling: 天长地久 (tiān cháng dì jiǔ).

Picture this. Look at the four characters one by one. 天 (tiān) means sky or heaven — something vast above you. 长 (cháng) means long or lasting. 地 (dì) means earth or ground — the solid world beneath your feet. 久 (jiǔ) means a long time. Put them together and you get a vivid image: "the sky is vast and long; the earth endures forever." Heaven and earth are the two most permanent things imaginable, so pairing them creates the ultimate picture of forever.

Simple rule. 天长地久 is a chengyu — a fixed four-character idiom that works as a single unit. You use it to describe anything that lasts as long as heaven and earth: deep love, enduring friendship, or timeless values. It often appears in romantic poetry and heartfelt wishes.

Try it yourself. Think of something in your life you hope will last forever — a relationship, a dream, a place you love. Write one sentence in English describing it, then try placing 天长地久 at the end as a poetic stamp: "Our friendship is 天长地久." Notice how those four characters carry a weight that would take a whole English sentence to match.
