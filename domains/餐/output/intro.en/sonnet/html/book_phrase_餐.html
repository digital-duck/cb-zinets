🌸 Phrase 餐

Think about the English word "break." Once you know it, you can start to decode breakfast (the meal that breaks your overnight fast), break time, take a break, and breakroom — all without memorizing each one from scratch. One familiar word unlocks a whole family.

Chinese works the same way. A phrase (短语, duǎnyǔ) is a short, fixed expression — usually two to four characters — that native speakers use as a single unit of meaning. The magic is that Chinese phrases are built from characters you can already recognize. When you know what a character means on its own, that knowledge travels into every phrase it belongs to.

Take the character 餐, meaning meal or to eat. It sits quietly inside 早餐 (breakfast), 午餐 (lunch), and 晚餐 (dinner). The first character in each pair tells you when — morning, noon, or evening — and 餐 tells you what — a meal. Two characters, one clear picture.

The simple rule: when you meet an unfamiliar phrase, look for a character you already know, use its meaning as an anchor, then let the surrounding characters fill in the details.

Try it yourself. You know that 餐 means meal. Can you guess what 用餐 (to use + meal) means? What about 餐厅 (meal + hall)? Read each pair aloud, trust your anchor, and see how far one character carries you.

Payoff 🎯

You have almost certainly used the word restaurant today — or at least thought about where your next meal is coming from. That small daily ritual is exactly what 餐 captures.

Picture a table set for dinner: dishes arranged, chopsticks placed, steam rising. 餐 holds that whole scene in a single character. It carries the idea of a meal — the food, the act of eating it, and the occasion that brings people together around it. You can spot it in 早餐 (breakfast, literally "early meal"), 午餐 (lunch), and 晚餐 (dinner). Add 厅, the word for a hall, and you get 餐厅 — a restaurant, a place whose entire purpose is the meal.

The simple rule: whenever 餐 appears, something about eating or a mealtime occasion is nearby. It anchors the event of nourishment, not just the food itself.
