🍃 容

Everyday example. Imagine you have a glass. You pour water into it — the glass holds the water. Now imagine you keep pouring and pouring until the glass simply cannot fit any more. That boundary — the moment a space runs out of room — is exactly what 容 (róng) is about.

Picture this. The character 容 is built from two parts: a roof on top (representing a house or shelter) and a shape below that suggests something filling that space. Think of a house that contains people, or a cup that holds liquid. 容 captures the idea of a space that welcomes something inside it.

Simple rule. 容 means to contain, to hold, or to tolerate. When something fits inside a space — physically or emotionally — 容 is the word for that relationship. In our idiom 水火不容, this single character carries the whole question: can these two things share a space? When the answer is no, 容 is the word being denied. The idiom turns on it.

Try it yourself. Look around the room. Find one container — a bag, a box, a bowl. Ask yourself: what does this hold, and what would it refuse to hold? That tension between "fits" and "doesn't fit" is the heart of 容. Can water and fire ever share the same bowl?
