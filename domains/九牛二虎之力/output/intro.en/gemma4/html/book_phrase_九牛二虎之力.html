🌸 Phrase 九牛二虎之力

Imagine you are moving a massive piece of furniture—say, an old china cabinet that weighs hundreds of pounds. If just one person tries to lift it, it's impossible. But if three or four strong friends work together, coordinating their movements and applying force simultaneously, suddenly the task becomes manageable. This incredible combined effort is what we describe with 九牛二虎之力 (jiǔ niú èr hǔ zhī lì). While literally translating to "the power of nine oxen and two tigers," this colorful idiom does not mean counting animals; instead, it describes an enormous, overwhelming amount of collective strength or effort—far greater than what a single person could achieve.

Picture this sense of scale: It’s not just strong effort, but the kind of concerted push that feels almost superhuman. When many people combine their individual efforts into one massive force, they create something greater than the simple sum of those parts. This phrase helps us give weight and importance to actions taken by a group, emphasizing that the combined energy is immense.

The simple rule for using this phrase is: Whenever you want to describe an effort so large, complex, and powerful that it requires many people working together in concert, remember 九牛二虎之力. It’s a grand way to praise tremendous collective accomplishment. Try it yourself by thinking of a major project—perhaps building a school or launching a huge company—and describing the sheer combined effort required using this phrase!
