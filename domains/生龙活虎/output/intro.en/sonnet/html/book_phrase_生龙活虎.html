Picture a living, leaping dragon and a tiger prowling with coiled strength. In Chinese tradition, the dragon (龙) is a symbol of soaring, supernatural vitality, while the tiger (虎) stands for raw, earthly power. Now pair them: both alive (生 / 活) and in full motion at the same moment. The image radiates unstoppable energy from every direction — nothing is holding either creature back.

Here is the simple rule at work: Chinese four-character phrases often stack two strong images side by side to multiply their force. Neither animal alone would be as vivid. Two creatures, two action words, one explosive impression — someone, or something, is bursting with life and vigor. The formula is concrete first (dragon! tiger!) and abstract second (vitality, health, drive).
