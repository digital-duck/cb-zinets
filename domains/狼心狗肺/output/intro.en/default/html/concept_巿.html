In ancient China, a small ornamental cloth pendant called fú (巿) served exactly that purpose: a cloth piece worn hanging from the belt as a symbol of rank or ceremony.

Picture this. Look at the shape of 巿. You can see what looks like a horizontal bar at the top — like the belt itself — with a cloth hanging down from the center. That drooping, fabric-like shape is the whole idea: something textile, something that hangs. It is one of the oldest "picture-words" in Chinese, a direct sketch of the object it names.
