🌸 Phrase 掩耳盗铃

Have you ever seen a toddler hide by covering their own eyes? They figure: if they can't see you, you can't see them. It is adorable — and completely backwards. We smile, because we know the hiding child is perfectly visible to everyone else.

Imagine a thief who wants to steal a large bronze bell from a temple courtyard. The moment he touches it, it clangs loudly. His brilliant solution? He presses both hands over his own ears. If he cannot hear the bell, surely no one else can! He reaches out, grabs the bell — clang! — and is immediately caught by the entire neighborhood.

That comic scene is exactly what the four-character phrase 掩耳盗铃 (yǎn ěr dào líng — "cover ears, steal bell") captures. The phrase is a shorthand label for a very human mental habit: pretending a problem does not exist by simply refusing to acknowledge it. Blocking your own perception does nothing to change reality for everyone around you.

The simple rule is this: awareness is not the same as existence. An inconvenient fact stays true whether you look at it or not.

Try it yourself. Think of one real-life situation — in school, at work, or in daily life — where someone (or even you!) acted as if ignoring a difficulty would make it disappear. What happened next? Recognising the pattern is the first step to breaking it.
