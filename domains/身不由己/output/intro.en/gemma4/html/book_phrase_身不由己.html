🌸 Phrase 身不由己

Imagine you are driving to a friend’s house, and suddenly, an accident blocks the entire highway. Even though your natural desire is to arrive quickly, your actions are limited by the massive traffic jam ahead. This specific feeling—of wanting to do something but being physically or circumstantially prevented from doing it—is what the phrase 身不由己 helps us express.

Picture this phrase as describing a powerful external constraint: when forces outside of our personal will dictate our actions. Literally, the characters suggest that one's "body cannot control itself." In plain language, we use 身不由己 when someone is compelled to act against their wishes due to circumstances (like traffic), rules (like workplace mandates), or obligations from others. It highlights a situation where free choice is unavailable.

The simple rule for using this phrase is: If an action happens to the person, rather than because they freely chose it, 身不由己 applies. The focus must always be on the external pressure or circumstance that causes the limitation. Using this phrase helps us express empathy by acknowledging that even good people sometimes lack full control over their lives.

To try it yourself, think of a time you couldn't go somewhere exactly when and how you wanted to. Instead of simply saying "I couldn't," practice describing the situation using this concept: What specific external factor made your desired action impossible?
