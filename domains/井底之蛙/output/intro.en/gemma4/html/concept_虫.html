Have you ever noticed how much life exists in a single fallen leaf? To an adult eye, it might just be decaying matter, but if you look closely, you see tiny networks of threads and miniature creatures. These small beings are what we call chóng (虫), which simply refers to insects or bugs. They seem insignificant compared to a grand forest view, yet they are the vital engine that keeps their immediate world running. They teach us that size does not determine importance; sometimes, the smallest elements hold the most complex and crucial systems.

Their entire worldview is built around their immediate neighbors and resources. They are masters of their small domain, but they have no concept of what lies beyond the edge of the leaf litter.
