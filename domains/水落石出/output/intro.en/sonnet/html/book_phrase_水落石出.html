In Chinese, 水 (shuǐ) means water, 落 (luò) means fall or recede, 石 (shí) means stone, and 出 (chū) means emerge. Put together: the water recedes, the stones come out.

Simple rule: 水落石出 describes any situation where confusion or concealment fades away and the truth finally becomes plain to see. Think of it as nature's own detective — patience and time reveal what was always there, just hidden.
