🍃 可

Think about something you want to do right now—maybe read a book or go for a walk. The ability to actually perform that action depends on certain things being true, right? This is where the concept of 可 comes in. Simply put, $\text{可}$ defines whether an outcome is possible or achievable under specific circumstances. It’s not about what is, but what can be.

Picture this: You want to make a cup of tea ($\text{Goal}$). To achieve that goal, you must have water ($W$), a teabag ($T$), and electricity to boil the water ($E$). If any one of these necessary components is missing—say, the kettle is broken (meaning $E$ is false)—then the whole action becomes impossible. $\text{可}$ helps us map out these prerequisites.

The simple rule for possibility can be written as a basic logical check: An Outcome $O$ is possible if and only if all its necessary Conditions $\{C_1, C_2, \dots, C_n\}$ are met. We can write this mathematically as: $\text{Possible}(O) \iff (\bigwedge_{i=1}^n C_i)$. This means that for $O$ to be true, all conditions must simultaneously be true.
