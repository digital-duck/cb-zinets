🌸 Phrase 前功尽弃

🌸 Phrase 前功尽弃

Everyday example: Imagine you spend three hours carefully building a tall tower out of wooden blocks. Just as you place the very last block, you bump the table and the whole tower crashes down. All that patient work — gone in an instant.

Picture this: The phrase 前功尽弃 (qián gōng jìn qì) paints exactly that scene. Break it apart: 前 means previous or before; 功 means effort or achievement; 尽 means completely or all; 弃 means abandoned or thrown away. Put them together and you get: "all previous effort completely abandoned." Every step you took before this moment has been wiped out.

Simple rule: Use 前功尽弃 whenever a final mistake or interruption erases the results of earlier hard work — not just a setback, but a total loss of accumulated progress. Think of it as a "reset to zero" moment. The emphasis is on the completeness of the loss: nothing from before survives.

Try it yourself: Picture a student who studies diligently for weeks, then accidentally submits the wrong file on exam day. Or a chef who tends a slow-cooked stew for hours, then knocks the pot off the stove. In both cases, would you say 前功尽弃 applies? Yes — because the accumulated effort produced no lasting result. Watch for that pattern: long effort + one undoing moment = 前功尽弃.
