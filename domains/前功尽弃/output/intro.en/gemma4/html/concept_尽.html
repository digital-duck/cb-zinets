🍃 尽

Have you ever seen a battery life indicator drop all the way down to zero percent? That moment—when there is absolutely no power left—is a perfect, everyday example of $\text{尽}$. Whenever we talk about something being used up entirely, or reaching its limit, that character comes into play. It describes the act of running things completely dry, whether it’s juice in your cup or energy in your body.

Picture this: Imagine filling a glass with water and then watching as you slowly pour it out until the bottom is bone dry. The moment the last drop leaves the container marks the point of $\text{尽}$. It doesn't just mean "almost empty"; it means finished, down to the absolute end. This concept of touching the final limit or completing a full cycle is what we are learning today.

The simple rule for understanding 尽 is this: if something reaches its maximum possible state, and then that state is fully depleted, you have reached $\text{尽}$. It always carries the sense of exhaustion or completion. When you see it in compound words, think "to the utmost degree" or "all gone."

To try it yourself, consider the phrase 极度 (jídù), which means extreme. Notice that '度' refers to a level or measure. By adding $\text{尽}$, we are emphasizing that this measurement is at its absolute maximum limit—the utmost degree. Keep practicing recognizing these signs of completion around you!
