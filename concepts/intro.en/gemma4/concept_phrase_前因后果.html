🌸 Phrase 前因后果

Imagine you are watching a row of dominoes fall. The first domino tips over, which causes the second one to tip, and so on, until the last one falls. This chain reaction is exactly what we mean by 前因后果 (qiányīn hòuguǒ), which simply means "cause and consequence." It is the ability to see how one event or statement logically leads to another. For instance, if you drop a glass of water (the cause), it will necessarily spill on the floor (the consequence).

Picture this concept as building with LEGOs: each piece must fit perfectly onto the one before it. If your initial premise is weak, the entire structure built upon it might wobble! Understanding qiányīn hòuguǒ means identifying those strong, logical connections between ideas—knowing that A leads to B, and B leads to C. We are looking for the causal pathway through an argument or story.

The simple rule is this: always ask "Why?" and "So what?". The thing you ask "Why?" about is the Antecedent (the cause), and the answer you receive is the Consequence (the result). To connect them, look for connecting words like therefore, because, or as a result. This structure ensures your argument flows smoothly and makes logical sense.

Try it yourself! When reading any explanation, pause after every sentence and ask: "What did this last idea make happen?" Mapping out those little cause-and-effect steps will help you understand the entire scope of the text much better.
