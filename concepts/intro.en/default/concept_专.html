🌱 专

Imagine a craftsman who spends every single day sharpening only one type of blade. He doesn't repair shoes, doesn't bake bread, doesn't fix carts — he sharpens blades, nothing else. Over years, his hands know the exact angle, the precise pressure, the right rhythm. That singular focus is what makes him extraordinary.

This is the idea behind 专: complete, undivided devotion to one thing. Think of it as a spotlight rather than a floodlight. A floodlight spreads its energy across a wide area, illuminating everything dimly. A spotlight pours all its energy into one small circle, making that spot brilliantly clear. 专 is the spotlight.

The simple rule: when attention, skill, or effort is gathered into one point without scattering, you have 专.

In the Chinese character 专, this concept lives right inside the brushstrokes — a spinning whorl that winds tightly inward, concentrating energy at its center rather than spilling outward. Chinese learners meet 专 in words like 专注 (focused attention) and 专家 (an expert — literally, "a 专 person"), but the concept itself belongs to any domain where singular dedication matters.

Try it yourself: Name one skill you have practiced so repeatedly that it feels nearly automatic. That accumulated, narrowed-down mastery is 专 in action. Now ask: what would your learning look like if you applied that same single-minded focus to one new thing for the next thirty days?
