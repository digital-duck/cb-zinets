🍃 散

Everyday example. Imagine a handful of dandelion seeds. The moment you blow, they drift apart in every direction — each seed finds its own path through the air. That moment of flying apart is exactly what 散 (sàn) captures.

Picture this. 散 means to scatter or to disperse — things that were together come apart and spread out. Think of a crowd leaving a stadium after a match: people who filled one space now fan out across the whole city. That spreading-out motion is the heart of 散.

In the phrase 不见不散, the character plays a promise-keeping role. 不 means "not," 见 means "meet/see," so the phrase reads literally as "not see, not scatter" — in other words, "I won't leave until I see you." Rather than drifting apart like those dandelion seeds, the two people stay put, waiting for the moment they find each other.

Simple rule. Whenever 散 appears, look for something moving from one place to many places — seeds, people, light, even a crowd's energy. The core idea is always: together → apart.

Try it yourself. Close your eyes and picture rain falling on a still pond. Each drop hits the surface and sends ripples spreading outward in every direction. Can you feel that outward movement? That's 散 in action. Next time you make plans with a friend, try saying 不见不散 — promise each other: neither of us leaves until we meet!
