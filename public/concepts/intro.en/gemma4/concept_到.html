🍃 到

Imagine you are waiting for a friend at a train station. You know they started somewhere else, but what matters right now is that they will eventually arrive here. This simple concept of arrival—the point where movement ends—is exactly what the character 到 represents. Think of it as marking the finish line on any journey, whether that journey is physical (like a train) or abstract (like finishing homework).

Picture this: Instead of just talking about movement, we are focusing solely on the destination. If you are traveling from Point A to Point B, "到" doesn't focus on how fast you went; it focuses only on having successfully reached Point B. It signifies completion and arrival at a specific location or state.

The simple rule is this: Whenever you want to indicate that something has achieved its endpoint—whether it’s time ($\text{time} \to \text{arrival}$), space ($\text{location A} \to \text{location B}$), or progress ($\text{start} \to \text{finish}$)—you use 到. It acts as a marker, confirming that the journey is complete at this point.

Try it yourself: Think about your favorite routine (like making coffee). Where does the process of "making coffee" finally reach its destination? The moment you smell the finished cup and are ready to drink it—that satisfying endpoint is what 到 helps us express!
