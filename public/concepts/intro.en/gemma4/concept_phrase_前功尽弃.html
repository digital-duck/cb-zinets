🌸 Phrase 前功尽弃

Imagine studying diligently for months—pouring countless hours into a massive project or exam. Then, just when you feel ready, an unexpected event forces everything to stop or requires a radical change that invalidates your preparation. This feeling of seeing all your hard work vanish is what the phrase 前功尽弃 captures. It describes the frustration and sense of loss when previous efforts are completely discarded or rendered worthless by a single setback.

Picture this: You spend weeks constructing an elaborate sandcastle, perfectly detailing every turret and wall. Just as you finish, a sudden wave rushes in, washing your entire masterpiece away. The "previous effort" (前功) was the careful building; the "discarding" (尽弃) is the moment of collapse. This phrase helps us acknowledge that setback without dwelling on the failure itself, recognizing the waste felt by the builder.

The simplest rule for using 前功尽弃 is this: Use it when an unexpected external force or sudden change invalidates a large amount of preparatory work previously invested. It always implies a significant sense of loss or regression from an advanced state. Remember that "前功" refers to accumulated progress, not just one single task.
