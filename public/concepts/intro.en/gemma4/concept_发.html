🌱 发

Think about describing an idea you just had. Perhaps you finally figured out how to solve a difficult puzzle, and it feels like the answer suddenly bursts into your mind. That initial burst—the moment of pure realization or creation—is what we mean when we talk about $\text{发}$ (fā). Conceptually, $\text{发}$ is the act of originating, releasing, or channeling internal energy or thoughts outward into the world. It isn't just having an idea; it is the active process of letting that idea take shape and be expressed.

Picture this: Imagine a garden hose pointed at dry ground. The water (your focused energy) must exit through the nozzle to do any work. If you keep the hose tightly coiled, all your potential energy remains trapped. $\text{发}$ teaches us how to open the spigot—how to release that pent-up mental or physical energy in a directed manner rather than letting it leak out inefficiently while you are distracted.

The simple rule of $\text{发}$ is this: Effective effort requires controlled emission. Instead of sporadic, scattered bursts of intense activity (which quickly leads to exhaustion), the goal is consistent, managed output. We aim for steady streams of focus over sudden torrents of panic.

To try it yourself today: Before you start a difficult task, take three deep breaths and visualize drawing your attention into a single, small point—like focusing on a tiny flame. From that controlled internal source, imagine releasing only the necessary energy required for the very next step, nothing more. This practice helps stabilize your $\text{发}$ (output) and prevents burnout.
