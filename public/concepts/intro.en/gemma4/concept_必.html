🍃 必

Think about a moment when you have to do something—like stopping at a red light or remembering your keys before leaving the house. That feeling of mandatory action is what the concept 必 captures. It doesn't just mean "maybe"; it means it must happen, or it absolutely needs to be done.

Picture this: Sometimes, necessity isn't about rules (like traffic laws), but about probability. If you are standing at the top of a very steep hill and there is nothing else below but a sheer drop, falling is practically unavoidable—that’s an example of inherent necessity. 必 helps us grasp that something is required or is destined to occur.

The simple rule for understanding 必 is this: if you can say "must," "definitely," or "it's unavoidable" in English, then the concept of 必 applies. It gives weight and certainty to a statement.

To try it yourself, think about something that happens every time you wake up (like needing to breathe air). What is one thing that must happen for your day to start? Recognizing that core necessity helps you understand when speakers or writers are using this powerful concept of inevitability.
