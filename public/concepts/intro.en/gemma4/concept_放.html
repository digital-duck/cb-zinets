🍃 放

🍃 放

Have you ever been carrying too many things—perhaps heavy books on one arm and worries about tomorrow in your pocket? When we talk about 放 (fàng), we are talking about release, which simply means letting go of something that is no longer serving us. Think of it like opening a tightly closed fist: you don't need to force the fingers open; you just need to stop squeezing. The physical act of dropping those books helps us understand the emotional idea: when you drop the burden, your hands become lighter and free for something else.

Picture this moment: Imagine you are holding onto a perfect memory—a beautiful sunset, perhaps—but you are also gripping it so tightly that it hurts your knuckles. 放 teaches us to let go of the grip on the memory, not the memory itself. We aren't discarding what happened; we are accepting that the moment exists fully and beautifully even if we don't have to physically hold onto it forever. This act of non-attachment is powerful because it allows peace to enter the space you previously occupied with tension.

The simple rule for 放 is this: Notice where you are holding tension, and choose to loosen your grip. Tension might look like constantly checking your phone, or maybe it sounds like an internal argument ("I should have done X"). When you identify that point of unnecessary pressure, remember that letting go isn't failure; it's energy conservation. You are giving yourself permission to simply be.

To try this out right now, pause for a moment and mentally list three things—commitments, worries, or self-criticisms—that feel heavy today. Now, visualize placing them gently on a pile outside of your mind. Do not solve them; just put them down. Notice the immediate, subtle shift in weight you feel in your chest. That lightness is 放 at work.
