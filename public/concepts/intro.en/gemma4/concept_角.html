🌱 角

🌱 Angle

Think about opening a book or standing in a corner of a room. The space created by those two meeting surfaces is what we call an angle. An angle ($\angle$) simply measures the amount of rotation between two lines that share a single point. This starting point, where the lines meet, is called the vertex (think of it as the corner). The straight paths forming the angle are called rays, and they stretch outward from that vertex. Don't worry about formulas yet—just picture the amount of "opening" between them!

Picture this: Imagine two rulers taped together so they form a letter 'V'. The space inside the 'V' is an angle. We write angles using three letters, like $\angle ABC$, where $B$ is always the vertex (the corner). When we measure it, we use units called degrees ($^\circ$). A perfect right corner, like the corner of a sheet of paper, is exactly $90^\circ$.

Simple Rule: An angle is formed by two rays that share a common vertex. The smaller the opening between them, the smaller the angle's measure! To find the size of an angle, you are finding how many degrees it has rotated from one side to the other.

Try it yourself: Look around your environment right now. Can you spot three objects that meet at a corner? Identify which point is the vertex and try to estimate if the angle formed there looks like $90^\circ$ or maybe a little wider! You are already exploring geometry just by observing your surroundings.
