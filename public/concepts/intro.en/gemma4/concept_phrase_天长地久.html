🌸 Phrase 天长地久

Have you ever heard someone speak about a promise or an affection that seems unbreakable, something meant to last forever? That feeling of permanence is captured by the phrase 天长地久 (tiān cháng dì jiǔ). On its face, it literally means "heaven [is] long" and "earth [is] lasting." But don't worry about memorizing two separate meanings! Instead, think of this phrase as a powerful idiom that describes something eternal—something that seems immune to time.

Picture the vastness above us (Heaven) and the solid stability beneath our feet (Earth). By combining these two grand concepts, 天长地久 suggests an existence or relationship that is unending and enduring indefinitely. It goes far beyond simply "very long"; it implies a permanence that defies human experience of time.

The simple rule to remember is this: If you want to describe something—a friendship, a commitment, or even a beautiful scene—that appears timeless, everlasting, or immutable, 天长地久 is your perfect choice. It carries a weight and grandeur that few other words can match.
