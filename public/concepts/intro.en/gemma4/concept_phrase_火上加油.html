🌸 Phrase 火上加油

Imagine you and a friend are having a small disagreement about whose turn it is to wash the dishes—a minor point of friction. If one person starts talking loudly and accusingly about everything else wrong, they aren't solving the problem; they are simply making the argument bigger. This action is exactly what 火上加油 (huǒ shàng jiā yóu) describes. It literally means "adding oil onto fire," and in conversation, it refers to the act of exacerbating a bad situation or conflict by adding unnecessary fuel.

Picture this: The initial disagreement about dishes is the small fire. If you respond by bringing up unrelated past mistakes (like a forgotten chore last week), that new input acts as the "oil." Instead of cooling down the argument, your comments only intensify the anger and make the whole situation much worse than it started. You haven't fixed anything; you’ve just increased the heat!

The simple rule to remember is this: When faced with an existing problem or conflict, if adding more words does not help resolve the issue, those words are likely acting as fuel. Understanding when to stop talking and let things cool down is a powerful communication skill.

Try it yourself: The next time you hear someone escalating a minor argument by bringing up old grievances, pause and ask yourself: Is this new information actually helping solve this current problem, or is it just adding more heat?
