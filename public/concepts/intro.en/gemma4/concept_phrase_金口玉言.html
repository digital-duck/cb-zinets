🌸 Phrase 金口玉言

Imagine you are giving advice to a close friend who is struggling. You could speak quickly and angrily, or you could take time to choose your words carefully, speaking with sincerity and thoughtfulness. That careful, heartfelt speech—the kind that helps someone grow without hurting them—is what we mean by 金口玉言 (Jīn Kǒu Yù Yán). Literally, it translates to "golden words from a jade mouth," suggesting speech that is both precious like gold and pure like jade. It doesn't just mean speaking nicely; it means speaking wisely and with genuine care.

Picture this: Your grandmother tells you a story about resilience. She doesn't lecture; instead, she shares her own minor mistakes and how she overcame them, using gentle metaphors. Her words are carefully chosen because she knows they will resonate deeply and help you understand life’s challenges better. This deliberate tenderness in communication is the core idea of Jīn Kǒu Yù Yán.

The simple rule here is: Before speaking, pause and ask yourself two questions: Is this true? And is this helpful? If the answer to both is yes, then your words are likely operating as 金口玉言. This practice requires deep self-awareness—the ability to regulate what comes out of your mouth.

Try it yourself: Next time you feel strongly about something and want to respond immediately, take a breath. Instead of giving an immediate reaction, try formulating a response that is empathetic (feeling with the other person) before you speak. This simple pause transforms careless words into precious advice.
