🍃 猴

Have you ever watched a large group of animals—or even people—react to something happening just out of sight? Maybe they all suddenly look in one direction, or perhaps they change their activity immediately after hearing a single sound. This shared state of attention and reaction is what we are exploring here. It shows how closely connected behavior can be within a community.

Picture this: Imagine a troop of monkeys observing something happening to one individual member of the group (like watching an interaction take place between two individuals). Even if the rest of the group was initially doing its own thing, their collective attention shifts completely. They are not reacting because of what they will do, but because of what they have just witnessed.

The simple rule for understanding "猴" is this: Group behavior often moves from individual action to shared anticipation. When one member experiences a clear consequence (a warning, a reward, or a loss), the entire group adjusts its internal rules and expectations based on that witnessed pattern. They learn what might happen if they behaved similarly.

Try it yourself: If you are in a large class and notice that whenever someone argues loudly, the teacher immediately gives a verbal warning to everyone (rather than just the person arguing), how do you think your behavior will change next time? You have learned a new group rule simply by observing the consequence of one action.
