🍃 至

Imagine you are hiking up a long trail toward a beautiful waterfall. You walk and walk, getting closer and closer until suddenly, there it is—the shimmering pool of water at the bottom. That moment when your feet finally reach the edge of the pool? That point is where the character 至 comes in. In simple terms, zhì (至) means "to arrive at" or "the ultimate limit." It marks the exact point of destination—it doesn't mean being close, it means being there.

Picture this concept moving from a physical hike to an abstract idea, like learning a new skill. If you practice coding for months, and finally write a program that works perfectly, the moment it executes flawlessly is your zhì. It’s not just "almost working"; it has reached its maximum potential. This character helps us pinpoint culmination—the successful endpoint of any process.
