🌱 长

Do you ever measure a piece of ribbon or draw an arrow across a page? When we talk about 长 (cháng), we are first learning about extension. In its most basic form, '长' simply describes something that stretches from one point to another over a measurable distance. It is the physical quality of being spread out—the difference between a short matchstick and a long rope.

Picture this: Now let’s move beyond objects and think about time. When we apply '长' to concepts, it means duration or permanence. If something is "long-lasting," we are talking not about its physical size, but about the continuous passage of its existence—like a river that flows endlessly through a valley, or a family tradition passed down for generations.

The simple rule to remember is this: '长' always describes continuation. It can be the length of a line segment (a spatial measure) or the duration of time (a temporal measure). Whenever you are assessing whether something extends—whether it’s $\Delta x$ in space, or $t$ over time—you are grappling with the idea of '长.'

Try it yourself: Think about your favorite memory. Does that memory feel brief, like a flash, or does it unfold slowly and stay vivid for a long period? The way you perceive its persistence is practicing the concept of ‘长.’
