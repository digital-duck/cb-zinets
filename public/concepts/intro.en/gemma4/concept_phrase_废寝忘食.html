🌸 Phrase 废寝忘食

Imagine a student cramming for major exams. They might spend three days straight in the library, fueled by coffee and sheer determination—pulling all-nighters and skipping proper meals just to absorb every last bit of textbook information. This dedication is what we are talking about when we use 废寝忘食 (fèi qǐn wàng shí). It refers to a state where one completely neglects their basic needs, like sleep (寢, qǐn) and eating (食, shí), because of intense focus on a goal.

Picture this: The phrase literally breaks down into "abandoning rest" and "forgetting food." It’s not simply about being busy; it describes an extreme state of deep engagement where the physical body takes a back seat to the mind's task. Think of mastering a difficult skill or working on a monumental project—the dedication level is intense, almost obsessive.

The simple rule here is this: While 废寝忘食 shows remarkable commitment and focus, it also warns us about burnout. It means dedicating yourself fully to something, but remember that even the most dedicated effort requires rest!
