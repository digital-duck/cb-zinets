🍃 容

Have you ever been trying to focus on a book, only for someone nearby to start playing loud music? It feels like an immediate conflict—you need silence, they are creating noise. In the context of "Water and Fire cannot coexist," this is where the concept of $\text{容}$ (Róng) comes in. Simply put, $\text{容}$ means tolerance or capacity; it is the ability to allow something different from you to exist without feeling the need to change it immediately.

Picture this: Imagine a clear glass bowl. You pour bright blue water into one side and then gently pour fiery red sand onto the other. They are fundamentally opposite—liquid versus solid, cool versus hot—yet they can simply sit next to each other in the same bowl. This space is $\text{容}$. It is not about them mixing together (which would be a different problem), but about acknowledging that both blue and red have their right to exist side-by-side without forcing one to dissolve into the other.

The simple rule of $\text{容}$ is this: Recognize difference first, react second. When you feel frustrated because someone's actions or beliefs clash with your own, pause. Instead of immediately fighting the difference (the "fire"), practice giving space and acknowledgment (the "water"). This act of non-judgmental acceptance is $\text{容}$.

Try it yourself next time you disagree strongly with a friend: Before formulating your rebuttal, take one deep breath and name the difference aloud—for example, "I see we approach this problem differently." That small action shifts the interaction from conflict to capacity.
