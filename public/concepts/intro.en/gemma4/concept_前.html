🍃 前

Imagine you are telling a friend a story about a favorite toy—say, a little red wagon. When your friend asks you, "What did you do with it?" Before you describe anything new, you must first remind them of its existence and what happened when you got it. That act of establishing the wagon's history is exactly what the concept 前 (preceding context) does. It acts like a gentle pause to confirm shared knowledge before moving forward.

Picture this: Think of your writing or speaking as a journey down a road. If you suddenly turn a corner without acknowledging the village you just left, your listener might get lost! The function of '前' is to look over your shoulder briefly and say, "Remember that we were talking about X? Well, because of X..." It anchors your audience firmly in what has already been said.

The simple rule for using 前 is this: If the idea you are about to present relies on a concept or piece of information that was discussed just moments ago, you must first reference it. You are drawing a clear line from the past point (the preceding one) to the future point (the new insight).

To try it yourself, take three simple sentences you wrote earlier today. Now, rewrite them so that each sentence explicitly reminds the reader of the core idea mentioned in the previous sentence. This practice will help you master the art of building solid contextual bridges!
