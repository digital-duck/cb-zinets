🌸 Phrase 显而易见

Imagine you are looking at a ripe apple on a branch; it’s clearly ready to be picked, right? This kind of undeniable truth—something that doesn't require deep thought or complex proof—is exactly what the phrase 显而易见 (xiǎn ér yì jiàn) describes. Think of this phrase as an emphatic signal that means "it is plain to see" or "obviously." We use it when we want our listener to accept a fact because it is so simple, it's impossible to miss!

Picture this: You and a friend are calculating the total number of fingers on two people. The answer is 10. You don't need an equation; you just look at your hands and instantly know the result. Using 显而易见 adds weight to that conclusion, telling everyone, "This isn't debatable—it’s obvious!"

The simple rule for using it is this: place 显而易见 directly before a statement or fact you want the listener to accept immediately without question. It functions like an intensifier, signaling that the evidence is right in front of your eyes. Remember, we are describing the evident nature of the statement itself.

Try it yourself! If someone suggests that water boils when heated, you can confirm this undeniable scientific fact by saying something like: "显而易见，水加热后会沸腾。" (It is obviously true that water will boil when heated.) You have successfully marked a piece of knowledge as universally evident!
