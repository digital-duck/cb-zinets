Have you ever been so absorbed in a book, a game, or a project that time vanished? This is what we mean when we talk about neglecting basic needs—it’s the state of being so deeply focused on one task that you forget to eat proper meals or get enough rest. It's natural! Our human brain is wired for intense focus. But while this dedication is powerful, pushing yourself too far into it can cause burnout and actually decrease your efficiency.

Picture this: Your brain is like a highly sophisticated battery-powered device. To perform complex tasks, it needs regular ‘recharges’ (sleep) and fuel (nutrition). When you ignore these basics, the amazing energy of your focus quickly drains, leading to fatigue or even poor decision-making. The simple rule here is that sustainability trumps intensity. You can maintain high performance not by working harder for longer, but by taking intentional breaks to refill your resources.

To try this yourself, implement a ‘micro-break’ timer. Every 45 minutes, set a reminder to stand up and move around for five minutes. This doesn't require leaving your study area—just stretch, grab water, or look out a window. By treating rest not as a reward, but as a mandatory part of the work process, you train your mind to perform optimally without sacrificing your well-being.
