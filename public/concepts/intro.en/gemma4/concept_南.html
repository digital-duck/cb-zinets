🍃 南

When we talk about direction, we often think of a compass pointing North, South, East, or West. The character 南 (nán) refers fundamentally to the geographical direction that lies below us—the south. Think about simple navigation: if you are standing in your kitchen right now and want to travel toward the equator, you are moving south. It describes everything located relative to a straight line running across the globe from north to south.

Picture this: Imagine drawing an invisible vertical line through any city on Earth. Everything that is physically situated below that line (in terms of latitude) belongs generally in the southern direction. This concept isn't just about geography; it can describe a spatial relationship—the "southern side" of a river, for instance.

The simple rule to remember is: 南 always denotes the relative position or movement toward the bottom portion of an area or map when oriented vertically. If you are discussing travel and see the phrase 走南闯北 (going through every corner), understanding 南 helps us pinpoint that journey's direction—it means traversing far into southern regions, showing comprehensive movement across vast distances.

Try it yourself: If you were looking at a map of your continent and wanted to find the warmest climate zone, you would likely be heading 南 (south). This fundamental concept of 'below' or 'downward' helps guide all geographical understanding.
