🍃 继

Imagine you are watching a team pass a baton in a relay race. The first runner runs hard, and when they reach the finish line marker, they smoothly hand off the baton to the next person. That seamless transfer—the act of one action immediately setting up the next—is the core idea behind 继 (jì). It doesn't just mean "next," it means following through or continuing from a previous point.

Picture this: Think about reading a book. Chapter 1 introduces the characters, and then Chapter 2 picks up exactly where Chapter 1 left off. The story flows forward because of that continuous connection. This relationship of seamless succession is what we call continuity. It’s not just two separate things happening; it's an unbroken chain.

The simple rule for 继 is this: If something follows something else, or if one idea builds directly upon the previous idea, you are seeing a pattern of 继. It emphasizes the connection and momentum between points in time or sequence. The first part (前) starts it, and 继 ensures that the action doesn't stop.

To try this yourself, look at your morning routine. What is the step that follows waking up? What comes after getting dressed? Identifying these linking steps helps you see how 继 works in real life—it’s the gentle push that keeps things moving forward!
