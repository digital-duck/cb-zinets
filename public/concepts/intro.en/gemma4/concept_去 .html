🌱 去

Have you ever needed to tell someone where you are going? That's where 去 ($\text{qù}$), which means "to go," comes in! Think of $\text{去}$ as a fundamental verb that describes movement from one place to another. It helps us talk about travel, whether it’s walking across the street or flying across continents.

Picture this: Imagine you are standing at your front door (Point A) and want to get to the bus stop (Point B). The act of going is the connection between those two points. $\text{去}$ captures that entire journey—the action of leaving one spot and heading toward another. It’s about transition!

The simplest rule for using $\text{去}$ is: If the subject (the person or thing doing the moving) is actively traveling, you use this structure:

$$ \text{Subject} + \text{去} + \text{Destination} $$

For example, if I say "I go to school," my journey is the action of $\text{去}$. The destination tells us where the movement ends.

Try it yourself: Think about your favorite local park or coffee shop. If you were telling a friend that you are going there right now, what would be the Subject, and what is the Destination? Try putting those pieces together using $\text{去}$!
