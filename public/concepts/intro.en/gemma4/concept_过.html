🍃 过

Imagine you are learning a new recipe: baking bread, for instance. You follow all the steps perfectly—the temperature is right, the kneading time is correct. This is 'enough.' But what if, because you love the smell so much, you keep leaving it in the oven for another hour just to make sure? That extra time is where we encounter the concept of 过 (guò), which simply means 'to cross' or, in this context, 'exceeding a limit.'

Picture this: Think of your talent like running on a graph. There is a sweet spot—the ideal practice time that maximizes learning without causing burnout. The line marking this perfect zone is the boundary. When you pass that invisible line and move into 过 (excess), you are no longer optimizing; you are overdoing it. This doesn't mean failure, but it means your method has crossed a point of diminishing returns—the effort outweighs the gain.

The simple rule for understanding when something is in 过 is to ask: "Is this extra amount making me better, or just more tired?" If the answer is 'more tired,' you have crossed the limit. Mastery isn't about doing more; it's about being balanced at the right point $x$.

Try it yourself: Think of a skill you are currently developing. Identify one area where you feel like you spend too much time or effort simply because you love it. How can you pull back just slightly—just enough to step away from 过 and find your optimal zone?
