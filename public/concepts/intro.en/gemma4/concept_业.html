🌱 业

Imagine you are baking cookies for a friend. If you carefully measure ingredients and follow the recipe—that is your action. The delicious, edible cookie that comes out of the oven—that is the result. Yè (業), often translated as Karma, simply means this universal law of action and consequence. It suggests that every intention we hold and every physical deed we perform creates a ripple effect in our lives.

Picture this law not as fate, but like tossing a stone into a still pond. The moment the stone hits the water is your action; the expanding circles are the consequences, which eventually reach you. These ripples show that nothing happens by accident—everything has an origin point. To understand Yè is to realize that we are perpetually co-creators of our own experience.

The simple rule is: Every seed planted (your actions and thoughts) will grow into a corresponding harvest (your experiences). This doesn't mean every single negative thought leads to disaster, but rather that habitual patterns—whether good or bad—tend to repeat themselves until we consciously change the pattern.

To try it yourself today, observe just one small action: when you feel frustrated with something, instead of immediately reacting, pause and identify two potential actions you could take instead. By consciously choosing your first reaction, you are planting a new, positive seed for tomorrow.
