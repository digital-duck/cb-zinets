🌸 Phrase 后来居上

Imagine you join a swimming club six months after your friends. They have a head start — they know the strokes, they've built up stamina. But you train hard every single day. By the end of the year, you're the fastest one in the pool.

That's the heart of 后来居上 (hòu lái jū shàng), which means: the latecomer ends up on top. The whole phrase works as one idea — someone who arrives later than everyone else eventually claims the highest position. You don't need to disassemble it to feel its meaning; just hold onto that image of finishing first despite starting last.

Picture a race where the runner in last place slowly, steadily overtakes everyone — not because the others were weak, but because this runner kept pushing past the finish line others thought was good enough. 后来居上 celebrates exactly that reversal of expectations.

Simple rule: whenever a newcomer or a later effort ends up outperforming what came before it, you can reach for 后来居上. It fits a younger sibling who earns higher grades, a startup that beats an older company, or a student who eventually surpasses the teacher.

Try it yourself: think of one area in your own life — a skill, a hobby, a subject at school — where you started later than others but caught up. What did "arriving later" actually give you — fresh eyes, stronger motivation, better tools?
