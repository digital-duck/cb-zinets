🌱 礻

Everyday example. Picture an old family altar table, with a tall plate standing on it where offerings of food and wine are placed for ancestors or gods. That picture — a plate raised for an offering — is the seed of a small but very important Chinese character piece called 礻.

Picture this. 礻 is not usually a full character on its own in modern writing. It is a "radical," which simply means a building-block piece that sits on the left side of many characters and gives you a hint about what the character means. Whenever you see 礻 on the left of a character, think: "this might be connected to gods, spirits, rituals, or worship." It comes from an older, fuller picture of an altar table (示), simplified down to three strokes for everyday writing.

Simple rule. Spot 礻 → think "something spiritual or sacred is nearby." For example, characters built with 礻 often relate to blessings, ancestors, ceremonies, or fortune-telling — because the original altar-table image is quietly tucked inside them.

Try it yourself. Next time you see a character with three short strokes stacked on the left side, pause and ask: "Could this be about spirits, luck, or ritual?" Try writing 礻 by itself a few times — a short hook, then a diagonal stroke, then a dot — until the shape of "the little altar" feels familiar in your hand.
