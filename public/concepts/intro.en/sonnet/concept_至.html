🍃 至

🍃 至

Imagine an arrow flying through the air and finally hitting the ground — it has arrived. That moment of landing is exactly what the character 至 (zhì) captures.

Picture this. The ancient form of 至 looks like an upside-down arrow with a short horizontal line beneath it. The line is the ground; the arrow is pointing straight down at it. Together they show the instant something reaches its destination — the journey is complete, the point has been touched.

Simple rule. 至 carries two related meanings that grow from the same image. First, it means to arrive at or to reach: 至今 (zhì jīn) means "up to now" — time has arrived at the present moment. Second, because arriving at a limit means you can go no further, 至 also means utmost or extreme: 至少 (zhì shǎo) means "at least," the smallest possible amount — the very bottom of a range.

Both meanings share one idea: something has gone as far as it can go.

Try it yourself. Look at the idiom 马到成功 (mǎ dào chéng gōng) — "the horse arrives and success follows." Now substitute 至 for 到: they are cousins, both meaning arrival. Can you spot 至 hidden inside 室 (shì, "room") — a place you arrive at and stay under a roof? Once you see the arrow landing, you will find 至 everywhere.
