🌸 Phrase 七嘴八舌

Think of the last time a group of friends tried to pick a restaurant. One person wants noodles, another insists on pizza, a third is already halfway through a review on their phone, and two more are arguing about parking. Everyone is talking, nobody is listening, and the noise just keeps growing. That moment has a name in Chinese.

Imagine ten people crammed around a table, each leaning in to say their piece at the exact same instant. You see mouths opening, tongues moving, hands waving — a blur of overlapping voices where no single thread can be followed. The room is alive with sound, but nothing is actually getting decided.

七嘴八舌 (qī zuǐ bā shé) captures this perfectly. The phrase pairs two small numbers — seven (七) and eight (八) — with two parts of the speaking body: mouth (嘴) and tongue (舌). The numbers aren't meant to be counted literally; they signal a whole bunch of. Mouth and tongue together point to speech itself. The result is a vivid shorthand: so many mouths, so many tongues, all at once — everyone talking over everyone else in cheerful or chaotic disorder.

The simple rule: when a crowd is buzzing with simultaneous voices and nothing is settled, 七嘴八舌 is the phrase that fits.

Try it yourself: picture a classroom the moment a surprise quiz is announced. Students whisper, groan, and protest all at once. Would you reach for 七嘴八舌? You absolutely would.
