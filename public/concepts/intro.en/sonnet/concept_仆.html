🍃 仆

Imagine a relay race where the lead runner stumbles and pitches forward — not sideways, not backward, but forward, still hurtling toward the finish line. That image of a person falling forward is exactly what the character 仆 (pū) captures.

Picture this: 仆 is built from two smaller pieces. On the left sits 亻, the person radical — a stripped-down sketch of a human figure standing upright. You will see 亻 on the left side of many characters involving people and their actions; it is one of the most common building blocks in Chinese writing. On the right, a short vertical stroke with a small tick leans forward, giving the whole character a sense of something tipping. Put them together and you see a person caught in the act of pitching ahead.

Simple rule:

$$ \text{仆} = \underbrace{\text{亻}}_{\text{person}} + \text{right side} \;\longrightarrow\; \text{a person falling forward} $$

In our idiom 前仆后继 (qián pū hòu jì), 仆 plays a precise role: those in front (前) fall forward (仆), and those behind immediately press on. The fall is not defeat — it is the very motion that keeps the chain moving.

Try it yourself: Write 仆 slowly. First draw 亻 on the left — a person standing tall. Then add the right side and let it lean slightly. Do you sense a figure caught mid-fall, still reaching ahead?
