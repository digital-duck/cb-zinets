🍃 弃

🍃 弃

Imagine you have been building a sandcastle all afternoon — piling up towers, smoothing walls, carving a tiny moat. Then, just before sunset, you kick it flat and walk away. That act of walking away, of intentionally letting go of something, is exactly what 弃 (qì) captures.

Picture this: 弃 is not losing something by accident. It is a deliberate choice to release, abandon, or discard. You decide to put it down and leave it behind. Think of throwing away a crumpled piece of paper — you held it, judged it, and chose to let it go.

Simple rule: Whenever 弃 appears, something is being voluntarily given up. The subject makes a conscious decision to abandon what was once in hand. No accident, no theft — just a willing release.

Now look at the idiom we are studying: 前功尽弃 (qián gōng jìn qì). Break it apart:

  • 前 = previous, before
  • 功 = achievement, effort, work done
  • 尽 = completely, all of it
  • 弃 = abandoned, discarded

Put it together: all previous effort, completely abandoned. Like that sandcastle — hours of careful work, gone in one moment of giving up too soon.

Try it yourself: Can you think of a time you nearly gave up on something right before finishing? That feeling — the weight of walking away from your own hard work — is the emotional heart of 弃.
