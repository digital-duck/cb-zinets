🍃 得

Everyday example. Imagine you water your garden and, while you're at it, you rinse off your muddy boots at the same time. One trip outside, two things taken care of. That satisfying feeling — "I got two things done at once!" — is exactly the spirit of 得.

Picture this. The character 得 looks like a hand reaching out and grasping something. At its heart, 得 means to obtain, to gain, or to achieve a result. It answers the question: what did you walk away with?

In the phrase 一举两得 (yī jǔ liǎng dé), 得 is the payoff:

$$ \underbrace{一}_{\text{one}} \; \underbrace{举}_{\text{action}} \; \underbrace{两}_{\text{two}} \; \underbrace{得}_{\text{gains}} $$

Read it left to right: one action → two gains. The 得 at the end is what you receive or accomplish as a result of that single effort.

Simple rule. Whenever you see 得 after a verb or action, ask yourself: what was gained? 得 points to the reward — the thing you successfully obtained or achieved.

Try it yourself. Think of a time you did one thing and got two benefits — maybe cooking dinner and using the leftovers for lunch the next day. You've just lived a 一举两得 moment! How many 得 (gains) can you count from your morning routine? Try listing them — you may surprise yourself.
