🌱 丨

Everyday example. Think about a tall, straight fence post planted firmly in the ground — it stands upright, confident, and unmoving. That single vertical line is exactly what you are looking at in the stroke 丨.

Picture this. In Chinese writing, every character is built from a small set of basic brushstrokes, much like how every house is built from a handful of shapes — walls, roofs, windows. The stroke 丨 (pronounced shù, meaning "vertical") is one of the most fundamental of all. It is a single, clean downward line, drawn from top to bottom in one smooth motion.

Simple rule. Whenever you write 丨, your brush or pen starts at the top and travels straight down. Think of it as drawing a $1$ on a piece of paper, but without the little foot or hat — just the bare vertical line itself. Formally, you can think of it as a line segment along the $y$-axis: moving in the direction $(0, -1)$ from start to end.

Try it yourself. Grab a pencil and draw five vertical lines, one after another, each about the same height. Try to keep them parallel and equally spaced. Notice how the simple act of drawing that single straight line already feels deliberate and intentional — that sense of purpose is exactly the spirit of 丨, the backbone hidden inside countless Chinese characters you will soon recognize.
