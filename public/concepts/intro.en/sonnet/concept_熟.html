🍃 熟

Think about the last time you made a recipe you had cooked many times before. At first, you had to check every step. But after making it over and over, your hands just knew what to do. You didn't even need to think — the whole process felt smooth and natural.

Picture this: a plum hanging on a tree. In the beginning it is hard and green. After days of sun and rain, it softens, deepens in color, and becomes fully ripe. That ripeness didn't happen instantly — it took repeated exposure, time, and the right conditions.

The character 熟 (shú) captures exactly that idea. It means ripe, cooked through, or thoroughly familiar. Whether you are talking about a peach that is ready to eat or a skill you have practiced until it feels effortless, 熟 describes something that has been fully processed — nothing raw or unfinished remains. In our idiom 熟能生巧, 熟 points to this state of deep familiarity with a task: you have done it so many times that it has "ripened" inside you.

Simple rule: When you see 熟, ask yourself — has this been fully worked through? If yes, that is 熟.

Try it yourself: Think of one skill you do without thinking today — tying your shoes, riding a bike, typing your name. At what moment did it become 熟 for you?
