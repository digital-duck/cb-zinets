🍃 由

🍃 由

Everyday example. Imagine you ask a friend, "Why are you late?" and they say, "It happened because of the traffic." That little phrase — because of, due to, from — is exactly what the character 由 (yóu) carries in Chinese.

Picture this. Look at 由 for a moment. It resembles a sprout pushing up through the top of a box, as if something is emerging from inside. That image captures its core feeling: something originates, rises, or flows from a source. In the idiom 火上加油, the oil (油, also pronounced yóu) pours fuel onto an already burning fire. Notice that 油 contains 由 inside it — 由 is the phonetic heart of 油, lending it both sound and a hint of "flowing from a source."

Simple rule. Whenever you see 由, think origin or cause. It answers the question "where does this come from?" In grammar, 由 often means from or because of: 由我来 means "let it come from me / I'll handle it." As a building block, 由 passes its sound (yóu) to characters like 油 (oil) and 邮 (mail).

Try it yourself. Cover the page and picture the sprout-in-a-box image. Now ask: what does 由 mean? What sound does it give to 油? Say both aloud — 由 (yóu), 油 (yóu) — and notice they rhyme perfectly. You've just found one of Chinese's most elegant patterns: a shared root, a shared sound.
