🌸 Phrase 呆若木鸡

Have you ever been so shocked that you couldn't move, speak, or even blink? Maybe you heard completely unexpected news, or watched something so astonishing that your brain simply froze. That frozen, speechless state is exactly what this phrase captures.

Picture this: A craftsman carves a rooster out of wood — smooth, still, completely motionless. Now imagine a real person standing with that same blank stare, not moving a muscle, as if they had turned to wood themselves. That image is the heart of 呆若木鸡 (dāi ruò mù jī).

Breaking it down character by character: 呆 (dāi) means stupefied or dazed; 若 (ruò) means "like" or "as if"; 木 (mù) means wood; and 鸡 (jī) means chicken or rooster. Put them together and you get: stupefied, as if a wooden chicken — frozen stiff with shock or awe.

Simple rule: Use 呆若木鸡 whenever someone is so startled or overwhelmed that they stand perfectly still with a blank expression, unable to react.
