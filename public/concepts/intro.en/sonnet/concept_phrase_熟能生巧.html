🌸 Phrase 熟能生巧

Everyday example. Think about the first time you tied your shoelaces. Every loop took concentration, and you probably had to slow down and look carefully at each step. Now you do it in seconds without even thinking. What changed? Not your hands — your practice.

Picture this. Imagine a potter at a wheel shaping clay. The first pot wobbles and collapses. The tenth looks better. The hundredth is smooth and even. Each repetition carves a tiny groove of understanding into the mind and the muscles, until the skill flows naturally.

Simple rule. The phrase 熟能生巧 (pronounced shú néng shēng qiǎo) captures this idea in four characters. Break it down word by word:

Character Sound Meaning
熟 shú familiar, well-practiced
能 néng can, is able to
生 shēng give rise to, produce
巧 qiǎo skill, cleverness

Read together: "Familiarity can give rise to skill." In everyday English: practice makes perfect. Repetition transforms something difficult into something fluent — that is the whole promise of this phrase.

Try it yourself. Pick one small thing you want to improve — writing a character, playing a chord, chopping vegetables. Do it slowly ten times today. Notice what becomes easier. That small shift is 熟能生巧 at work.
