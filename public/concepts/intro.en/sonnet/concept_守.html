🍃 守

🍃 守

Everyday example. Imagine you leave a friend in charge of your house while you travel. You ask them to stay put and make sure nothing goes wrong. That role — being the one who stays and watches over — is exactly what 守 (shǒu) means: to guard, to keep watch, to stay by something.

Picture this. Look at the character 守. The top part, 宀, is a little roof — think of it as a house. Underneath sits 寸, which originally pictured a hand held close to the body. Put them together: a hand under a roof — someone staying inside the home, keeping it safe. The image practically tells the story.

Simple rule. 守 carries the core idea of holding your position. It appears whenever someone (or something) stands watch over a place, an object, or even a habit. In our four-character phrase 守株待兔, this character sets the scene: a person who stays fixed beside a tree stump, refusing to move on.

Try it yourself. Say the word aloud: shǒu (falling-rising tone, third tone in Mandarin). Now trace the character slowly — roof first, then the hand beneath. Ask yourself: what are you guarding right now? A favourite spot? A daily routine? Any time you hold steady and keep watch, you are living the meaning of 守.
