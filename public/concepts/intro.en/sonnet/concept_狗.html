Have you ever watched a dog backed into a corner — maybe by another animal or a loud noise? It doesn't just cower. It scrambles, leaps, even clears a fence it would never bother with on a calm afternoon. That frantic energy is exactly what the character 狗 captures.

狗 (gǒu) simply means dog. In written Chinese, many characters carry a small clue called a radical — a recurring piece that hints at meaning. 狗 contains the radical 犭 (a simplified form of 犬, also meaning dog or canine). Think of the radical as a category label: whenever you spot 犭 on the left side of a character, animals — often four-legged ones — are usually involved.

Here is a simple rule worth remembering: 犭 on the left = likely an animal connection. The rest of the character adds the specific sound and meaning on top of that shared animal tag.

In our idiom 狗急跳墙, 狗 plays the starring role. It sets the whole scene: a dog (狗) is the creature whose desperation drives the action. Without understanding what 狗 means, the idiom's vivid image — a panicked animal launching itself over a wall — stays locked behind unfamiliar symbols.
