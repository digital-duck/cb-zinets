Have you ever watched a snake glide silently through tall grass? Its long, smooth body winds and curves as it moves — no legs, no arms, just one continuous flowing shape.

Picture this: Imagine drawing a single curving line on paper, letting it twist and loop gently. That winding line captures the essence of 蛇 (shé) — the Chinese character for snake.

The character 蛇 is built from two parts working together. On the left side sits 虫 (chóng), which originally meant "creature" or "crawling thing" — ancient Chinese used it for all manner of small animals and insects. On the right side is 它 (tā), which in classical Chinese actually meant snake before it shifted to mean "it" in modern usage. So 蛇 literally combines "creature" with an old word for snake itself — a fitting pair!

Simple rule: When you see 虫 on the left side of a character, it often signals the character relates to an animal, insect, or small living creature. 蛇 follows this pattern perfectly.
