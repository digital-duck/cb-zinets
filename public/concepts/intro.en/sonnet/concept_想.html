🍃 想

Have you ever been trying to decide something — say, whether to order pizza or noodles — and found yourself going back and forth in your head, picturing both options? That mental back-and-forth is exactly what 想 captures.

Picture this: The character 想 is built from two parts stacked on top of each other. The upper half is 相 (xiāng), which carries the idea of looking at something carefully, as if comparing two things side by side. The lower half is 心 (xīn), meaning heart — but in Chinese thought, the heart is also the seat of the mind. So 想 literally pictures the heart looking closely at something: thinking, imagining, longing.

Simple rule: Whenever you see 想, someone's mind is at work — forming a thought, conjuring an image, or wishing for something. It can mean to think, to imagine, or to miss (someone you care about), depending on context.

In our idiom 左思右想, you'll notice 想 appears alongside 思 (sī), another character for thinking. Together they double the sense of mental effort: thinking left, thinking right — turning a problem over and over until you've seen it from every angle.

Try it yourself: Can you think of a time you 想-ed hard before making a decision? What were the two "sides" your mind kept visiting?
