Everyday example: Think about a sunflower on a hot summer afternoon when it hasn't been watered. Instead of standing tall and proud, its heavy head bends downward, drooping toward the ground. That gentle, gravity-pulled motion — hanging down — is exactly what the character 垂 (chuí) captures.

Picture this: 垂 is a verb meaning to droop or to hang down. Imagine holding a piece of string with a small weight tied at the bottom. No matter how you turn it, the weighted end always falls — it hangs. The character 垂 describes that downward, suspended quality. You'll see it in phrases like 垂头 (chuí tóu), which means "to lower one's head," the very image of someone too sad or defeated to look up.
