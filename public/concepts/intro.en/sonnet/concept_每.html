🍃 每

🍃 每

Everyday example

Think about brushing your teeth. You don't do it just once — you do it every day, morning and night. In Chinese, the character 每 (měi) captures exactly this idea: "each one" or "every single one," with nothing left out.

Picture this

Imagine a row of identical lockers in a school hallway. If your teacher says "every locker must be checked," she means all of them, one by one, without skipping any. That's the feeling behind 每. It's not vague like "some" or "most" — it points to each individual item in a group, treated one at a time but covering the whole set.

You'll often see 每 paired with a measure word or noun right after it, like 每天 (měi tiān, "every day") or 每个人 (měi gè rén, "every person"). The character almost always introduces a phrase, setting up the idea of "for each X" before the rest of the sentence tells you what happens.

Simple rule

$$ 每 + (\text{measure word/noun}) = \text{"every"} + (\text{noun}) $$

So 每天 = "every day," 每次 = "every time," 每年 = "every year." The pattern stays consistent — just attach 每 to whatever unit you're counting.

Try it yourself

Pick a daily habit of yours — eating breakfast, checking your phone, saying hello to someone. Now say it in your head as "每天我..." ("Every day I..."). Notice how 每 instantly signals "this happens for all days, no exceptions."
