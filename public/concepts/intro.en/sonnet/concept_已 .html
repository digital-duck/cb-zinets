🌱 已

🌱 已

Everyday example. Imagine you ask a friend, "Have you eaten?" and she answers, "I already have!" That little word already tells you the action is finished — it happened before now, and there is no need to do it again. In Chinese, one small character carries exactly that meaning: 已 (yǐ).

Picture this. Look at the shape of 已. Many learners see a little hook curling back on itself, like a hand that has reached out, grabbed something, and drawn it in. The action is complete; the hand has returned home. That curling-back image captures the feeling of "done and settled."

Simple rule. 已 signals completion or a state that is already true. Whenever you see 已, ask yourself: Has this thing already happened? If the answer is yes, 已 is almost certainly nearby. Think of it as a small flag planted at the finish line, quietly announcing: done.

In our domain 日新月异 ("changing with every passing day"), 已 reminds us that yesterday's change is already behind us — today brings a new one. Each sunrise finds the world slightly different from what it 已 was.

Try it yourself. Think of one thing you finished today — breakfast, a phone call, a task. Say to yourself: "That is 已 done." Picture the little curling hook closing around it, sealing it as complete. Now think of something else that is already true in your world, and place 已 before it in your mind. Notice how one small character can mark the whole past as settled.
