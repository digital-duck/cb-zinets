🍃 路

Imagine you're walking to a friend's house. You don't just teleport there — you follow a path: down your street, past the corner store, across the bridge. That path, that "way to get somewhere," is exactly what the Chinese character 路 (lù) means: a road, a path, or a way.

Picture this: 路 is built from two parts. On the left is 足 (zú), a simplified picture of a foot — because roads are originally things you walk on. On the right is a second piece that doesn't show a new picture to learn; instead, it just gives the character its sound, "lù." So really, only one part carries meaning here: the foot. Put it together, and you get a character that literally carries the idea of "feet finding their way."

Simple rule: Whenever you see 路 in a word, think "route" or "journey." For example, in the phrase 一路平安 (yī lù píng ān) — "may your whole journey be peaceful" — 路 marks the entire trip, not just the road surface. It's the experience of traveling, start to finish.

Try it yourself: Next time you say goodbye to someone heading on a trip, try saying "一路平安!" Picture their whole path — every step, every mile — wrapped in safety. That mental image is 路: not a static object, but a living stretch of travel connecting one place to another.
