🍃 意

Everyday example. Imagine you're trying to guess what your friend is really thinking when they give you a strange smile. You can't see their thoughts directly — but somehow, you sense a meaning behind their expression. That hidden meaning, the "what's really going on inside," is close to what the Chinese character 意 (yì) points to.

Picture this. 意 is built from two parts stacked together: 音 (yīn, "sound") sitting on top of 心 (xīn, "heart" or "mind"). Think of it as sound rising from the heart — the idea that what's inside your mind eventually wants to come out, like a feeling pressing upward until it becomes a thought, a word, or an intention. 意 means "intention," "meaning," or "idea" — the inner sense behind an outward expression.

Simple rule. Whenever you see 意, think: "there's a meaning or intention underneath." It rarely stands alone in modern Chinese — it usually teams up with other characters to form words, such as 意思 (yìsi, "meaning") or 注意 (zhùyì, "pay attention"). The character itself is like a signal flag: "something deeper is meant here."

Try it yourself. Look at the phrase 一心一意 (yì xīn yí yì), meaning "wholeheartedly, with single-minded devotion." Notice how 意 pairs with 心 (heart) again — reinforcing that true intention comes from a heart and mind aligned as one. Can you think of an English phrase that captures "putting your whole heart and mind into something"? That's the spirit 意 carries.
