🌱 攵

Imagine a teacher tapping a student's shoulder to get their attention, or a parent gently patting a child's hand to guide them. That small, deliberate action — a controlled tap or strike — is exactly what the component 攵 (pronounced roughly like pū) captures.

Picture this: 攵 looks like a hand holding a short stick, poised to give a gentle knock. In the ancient pictograph, you can almost see an arm swinging downward in a purposeful, light motion. It is not a violent blow; it is a directed, intentional touch.

Simple rule: Whenever you spot 攵 on the right side of a character, think guided action — someone is doing something with purpose and force. 攵 acts like a signal that says, "a hand is involved, and something is being set in motion." In the character 放 (fàng, to release or set free), 攵 combines with 方 (a direction marker) to suggest directing something outward — nudging it loose, letting it go. That is the heart of 解放: first 解 unties the knot, then 放 (with its 攵) pushes the thing free.

Try it yourself: Look at the character 改 (gǎi, to change) and 收 (shōu, to collect). Can you spot 攵 in each one? Notice how both involve a deliberate human action — correcting something, or gathering something in. 攵 is the silent reminder that a hand made this happen.
