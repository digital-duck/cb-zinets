🌱 见

🌱 见

Have you ever walked into a room and instantly noticed someone — before they even said a word? That moment of seeing is exactly what the character 见 (jiàn) captures.

Picture this: Imagine a person standing upright, with one enormous eye at the top. Ancient Chinese scribes drew 见 almost exactly that way — a figure whose entire upper body is an eye. The character is telling you: this is a being defined by the act of looking.

Simple rule: 见 means to see or to meet — and in Chinese, those two ideas are deliciously linked. When you truly see someone, you are also meeting them; when you meet someone, you are truly seeing them for the first time.

In our phrase 一见钟情 (yī jiàn zhōng qíng — "love at first sight"), 见 is the electric moment: one glance, and everything changes. It is the hinge the whole phrase swings on.

You will find 见 hiding inside many everyday words:

Word Pinyin Meaning
见面 jiànmiàn to meet face-to-face
看见 kànjiàn to catch sight of
意见 yìjiàn opinion (ideas you "see")

Try it yourself: Think of the last time something caught your eye completely by surprise. That instant — that is 见.
