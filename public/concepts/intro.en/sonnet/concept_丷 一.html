It means one, and it is the simplest character in all of Chinese writing. Now look at 丷: two tiny, almost mirror-image strokes sitting side by side, like two small seeds or two drops of rain just starting to fall. Simple rule: When 丷 rests above 一, picture a pair of seeds planted on flat ground. These two components often appear together as the upper portion of a larger character.
