🍃 小

Imagine holding a grain of rice between your fingers. It is tiny — easy to overlook, easy to drop. Yet without it, there is no meal. That grain of rice is the perfect spirit of 小.

Picture this. The character 小 (xiǎo) looks like three short strokes: one small vertical line in the center, with two even shorter dots leaning away on either side. Visually, the center stroke is already modest, and the side strokes are smaller still — the shape itself whispers "little, little, littler."

In Confucius's warning — learning without thinking is wasted effort — every great insight starts from something 小: a single question, a single word, a single character. The character 小 reminds you that small steps are not weak steps. They are the beginning of everything large.

Simple rule. 小 means small or little. It appears in everyday words: 小心 (xiǎo xīn, "be careful" — literally "small heart," meaning pay close attention to tiny details) and 小学 (xiǎo xué, "elementary school" — the small steps of learning).

Notice: 学 (to learn) contains the idea of building up from small pieces, just as 小学 names the place where that building begins.
