🌸 Phrase 自食其力

Think about a teenager who starts doing odd jobs around the neighborhood — mowing lawns, walking dogs — so they can buy their own video games without asking their parents for money. They earn what they spend, and that small act of independence feels genuinely satisfying.

Now picture a farmer who grows her own vegetables, trades some at the market, and uses what she earns to buy everything else she needs. Every meal on her table traces back to her own hands. No one handed it to her; she made it happen herself.

This is the spirit captured by 自食其力 (zì shí qí lì) — a four-character Chinese phrase meaning to provide for oneself through one's own effort and labor. Break it down: 自 (zì) means "self," 食 (shí) means "to live on" or "to eat from," 其 (qí) points back to "one's own," and 力 (lì) means "strength" or "effort." Put together: live on one's own strength. Any time someone earns their keep without relying on handouts or outside support, that is 自食其力 in action.

Can you think of a moment when you handled something entirely on your own — earning money, cooking a meal, solving a problem without asking for help? That feeling of quiet pride is exactly what 自食其力 names.
