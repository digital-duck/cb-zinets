🌱 母

Everyday example

Think about the word mother in English. Now look at the Chinese character 母 ($m\check{u}$), which also means "mother." Just like English has the word "mom," Chinese has 母 as a building block that appears inside many other characters and words related to family, care, and origin.

Picture this

Imagine the character 母 as a simplified picture of a mother — some scholars say the two dots inside represent a mother nursing a child, a symbol of nurturing and giving life. This idea of "the source that nourishes" is why 母 shows up in words like 母语 ($m\check{u}y\check{u}$, "mother tongue," your native language) and 母校 ($m\check{u}xi\grave{a}o$, "alma mater," your home school).

Simple rule

Whenever you see 母 in a word, think "origin" or "nurturer." It signals something foundational — like a mother is foundational to a family. This single character, $\text{母}$, carries that root meaning wherever it travels into new combinations.

Try it yourself

Look at these two words: 母亲 ($m\check{u}q\bar{\imath}n$, "mother") and 父母 ($f\grave{u}m\check{u}$, "parents"). Notice how 母 always points back to the idea of a caring source — whether it's a person, a language, or a school. Next time you spot 母, ask yourself: "What is this the origin of?"
