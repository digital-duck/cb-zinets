🍃 夆

Everyday example: Imagine two friends walking toward each other from opposite ends of a street. The instant they spot one another and step forward to meet is a small but meaningful event. Chinese writing captures that exact moment of coming together in a single building block: 夆 (féng).

Picture this: Look at 夆 carefully. At the top sits 丰, a character evoking lushness and outward branching — picture a plant sending shoots in every direction. Beneath it is 夂 (suī), a stylized drawing of a foot stepping forward. Put them together and you have an image of something reaching out and arriving: an encounter already in motion.

Simple rule: 夆 rarely appears alone in modern Chinese. Its main job is to act as a phonetic component — the part of a larger character that hints at pronunciation. Whenever you spot 夆 tucked inside another character, expect a sound close to féng. The most important example is 逢 (féng, to encounter, to meet), which pairs 夆 with the movement radical 辶 (a road being traveled). So:

$$ \text{辶} + \text{夆} = \text{逢} $$

Someone traveling a road and meeting someone along the way.

Try it yourself: Return to the idiom 左右逢源. Find 逢 in the middle. Now look inside 逢 — can you spot 夆 hiding there? You have just located the sound-carrier, the tiny heartbeat of "meeting," embedded at the core of the whole phrase.
