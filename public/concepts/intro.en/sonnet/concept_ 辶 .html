Everyday example: Imagine stepping outside and walking down the street — you leave your doorstep, move along a path, maybe round a corner, and keep going. That simple act of traveling through space is something Chinese captures with a single brushstroke building block.

Picture this: The shape 辶 looks a little like a dotted trail with a gentle sweep beneath it. Think of it as a stylized footpath seen from above: a small dot marks the first step someone has taken, and the curving stroke below traces the road stretching ahead. Calligraphers sometimes call it the "walk" radical.

Simple rule: A radical is a repeated visual piece inside Chinese characters that hints at shared meaning — much like knowing the English root -port- ("carry") helps you guess "transport" or "portable." Whenever you spot 辶 nestled under or beside another component, it signals that the word involves movement, travel, or direction. It's the character's quiet way of saying: something is going somewhere here.
