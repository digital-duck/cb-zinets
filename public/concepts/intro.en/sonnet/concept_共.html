🌱 共

Imagine a group of friends splitting a pizza. Each person at the table gets a slice — nobody owns the whole pie, but everyone shares in it together. That shared pizza is the core idea behind 共 (gòng): together, shared, or in common.

Picture this: Draw a small shape, then picture two hands holding it from below, supporting it jointly. Neither hand alone could keep it steady — both are needed. That joint effort, that mutual holding, is what 共 captures visually. The bottom strokes of the character suggest exactly this: something being upheld by more than one.

Simple rule: Whenever you see 共, think held together by all. It signals that something is shared, joint, or communal — not belonging to one person but to a group acting as one.

In Chinese, 共 appears in words like 共同 (gòng tóng, "jointly") or 共享 (gòng xiǎng, "shared enjoyment"). But the concept travels far beyond language: you encounter it in mathematics (a common factor), in law (a joint account), and in everyday life (a shared goal).

Try it yourself: Think of three things in your life that are truly shared — not yours alone, not someone else's, but held in common. A neighborhood park, a family recipe, a team's trophy. Each one is a real-world example of 共 in action.

The character is a gentle reminder: some things only work when held together.
