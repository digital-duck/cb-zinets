🍃 会

🍃 会

Think about the last time you said goodbye to a friend and added, "See you around!" That little phrase carries a quiet confidence — you believe a future meeting is coming, even without a fixed date.

Picture this. In the idiom 后会有期, the four characters translate roughly as:

$$ \underbrace{后}_{\text{later}} \quad \underbrace{会}_{\text{will meet}} \quad \underbrace{有}_{\text{there is}} \quad \underbrace{期}_{\text{a time}} $$

The whole phrase means "There will be a time we meet again." The character at the center of that promise is 会.

Simple rule. 会 (huì) does two related jobs in Chinese. First, it expresses ability — something you have learned or mastered, like I can swim. Second, and more relevant here, it signals a likely future event — something you expect to happen. You can think of both meanings as pointing forward: a skill you carry into the future, or an event you anticipate ahead. In 后会有期, 会 carries that second sense: will, expect to, is going to.

Try it yourself. Cover the translation above and read 后会有期 aloud slowly. Ask yourself: which character is making the promise that something will happen? That one — 会 — is the engine of the whole farewell. Notice how much warmth a single character can hold.
