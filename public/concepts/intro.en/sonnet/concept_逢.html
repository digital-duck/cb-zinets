🍃 逢

Imagine you are walking through a busy market and you unexpectedly bump into an old friend. That happy, unplanned meeting — that moment of "oh, it's you!" — is exactly what 逢 (féng) captures.

Picture this: You are strolling down a path (notice the walking radical 辶 on the left side of the character — it looks like someone moving forward). As you walk, you encounter something or someone. The character 逢 always carries this sense of coming across something while in motion. It is not a planned appointment; it is a meeting that happens naturally along the way.

Simple rule: Whenever you see 逢, think "to meet" or "to come across" while moving through the world. It describes an encounter — with a person, an opportunity, or even a lucky moment — that arrives as you go about your journey.

Now look back at our idiom 左右逢源 (zuǒ yòu féng yuán): left and right, [you] encounter sources. The 逢 here tells us that on both sides of the path, good things keep appearing — not because you forced them, but because you naturally meet them as you move.

Try it yourself: Think of a time you unexpectedly ran into a friend or stumbled upon something useful. In Chinese, you might say you 逢 them — you encountered them along the way. How does that feel different from a planned meeting?
