🌱 亅

Imagine you are fishing. You drop a line straight down into the water, and at the very tip hangs a small hook that curves back up to catch a fish. That single image — a straight line with a tiny upward curl at the bottom — is exactly what the stroke 亅 looks like.

Picture this: draw a vertical line downward, as if you are writing the number 1. Just before you lift your pen, flick the tip gently to the left. That little flick is the hook. The whole mark is called jué (亅), and it is one of the most fundamental strokes in written Chinese.

Simple rule: 亅 is not a stand-alone character you will read in a sentence. It is a radical — a building-block piece that combines with other strokes to form full characters. Think of it like the letter "l" in English: on its own it means little, but inside words like "play" or "left" it does important work. You will find 亅 hiding inside characters such as 了 (le, completion) and 事 (shì, matter/affair).

Try it yourself: Pick up a pen and practice the stroke three times: down, then hook left at the bottom. Keep the hook small — about one-fifth the length of the vertical line. Notice how a tiny curve completely changes the feel of the mark. That sensitivity to small details is at the heart of learning to read and write Chinese.
