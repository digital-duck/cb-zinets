🍃 到

🍃 到

Everyday example. Imagine you are waiting for a friend at a café. The moment they walk through the door, you say "You made it!" That arrival — the crossing of a threshold — is exactly what 到 (dào) captures.

Picture this. In our phrase 马到成功, the warrior's horse is galloping toward the battlefield. The instant the horse arrives — hooves touching the ground — victory follows. 到 sits right in the middle of the phrase, acting like the hinge on a door: before it is the subject in motion (马, the horse), after it is the outcome (成功, success). Arrival triggers the result.

Simple rule. 到 most often means "to arrive at" or "to reach." Think of it as marking the moment a journey ends and something new begins. In grammar, it also works as a result complement — a small word attached to a verb to show the action was completed successfully. So 做到 (zuò dào) means "to do and actually accomplish it," not just to try.

Try it yourself. Picture these two scenes and notice the difference:

  • 他来了。 — He came. (the action happened)
  • 他来到了。 — He arrived here. (the destination was reached)

Can you feel how 到 adds that extra sense of landing? Every time you see it, ask: what threshold just got crossed?
