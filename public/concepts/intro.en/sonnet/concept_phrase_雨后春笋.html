Everyday example: Imagine you wake up after a warm spring rain and walk into your backyard. Yesterday the ground looked bare. Today, dozens of bamboo shoots have pushed up overnight — green, tender, and everywhere you look.

Picture this: Spring bamboo (春笋, chūn sǔn) is famous for one astonishing trick: after rain (雨后, yǔ hòu — literally "after rain"), it doesn't grow slowly. It erupts. A single shoot can rise several centimeters in a single day. The whole patch transforms almost before your eyes.

Simple rule: 雨后春笋 (yǔ hòu chūn sǔn) describes anything that appears suddenly in great numbers, the way spring bamboo shoots burst from the soil after rain. Break it down: 雨 = rain, 后 = after, 春 = spring, 笋 = bamboo shoots. Together they paint a vivid picture — rapid, abundant, unstoppable growth.

You'll hear this phrase whenever something new multiplies fast: coffee shops opening across a city, apps launching after a tech trend, volunteers signing up for a cause.
