🌱 门

Every day you walk through doors without thinking twice — the front door of your home, the gate of a school, the entrance to a shop. That ordinary act of passing through an opening is exactly what the Chinese character 门 (mén) captures.

Picture this. Look at 门 closely. You can almost see two panels of a swinging double door, side by side, joined at the top by a lintel. Ancient scribes drew it exactly that way — a rectangular frame with two leaves hanging inside. Over centuries the brushstrokes rounded and simplified, but the image stayed: an opening you step through.

Simple rule. 门 means door or gate, and it does two jobs in Chinese. First, it stands alone as a word: 门 = door. Second, it lives inside other characters as a building block, hinting that the larger character has something to do with an opening or passage. When you spot 门 tucked inside a more complex character, ask yourself: Is there a door, a gap, or a way in hidden here?

Try it yourself. Think of the idiom we are exploring — 走南闯北, traveling boldly in every direction. Imagine a traveler pushing open one gate after another, north and south, east and west. Each 门 is a new threshold. Now look at the character 闯 (chuǎng, "to forge ahead boldly") — do you see 门 sheltering a horse inside? The traveler charges right through the gate!
