🌱 孚

Think about the moment a hen sits on her eggs, day after day, radiating steady warmth until the chicks inside finally crack through the shell. Nothing dramatic happens from the outside — just patient, faithful presence doing its quiet work. That image of a claw gently covering something precious, keeping it safe and warm until trust is earned, is exactly the idea behind 孚.

Picture this: You borrow a friend's car. They hand over the keys not because you signed a contract, but because you have shown, over time, that you take care of things. That transfer of the keys is 孚 — trust that has been incubated through consistent, reliable behavior until it hatches into action.

The simple rule: 孚 names the kind of trust that is earned by tending, not declared or demanded. It lives halfway between faithfulness (what you do) and credibility (what others believe about you). You cannot shortcut it; you can only sit on it, like the hen, until it ripens.

In the Chinese character 孚, the upper part suggests a claw or hand hovering protectively, and the lower part is a child — captured and kept safe. The whole picture says: to hold something so carefully that it grows to trust you.

Try it yourself: Name one relationship in your life where trust grew slowly through repeated small acts. What was the "egg"? What was the "warmth" you provided? That slow ripening is 孚 in practice.
