🌱 雨

Everyday example. Think about the last time it rained. Droplets fell from the sky, collected on window ledges, and dripped steadily to the ground. That scene — clouds above, water falling — is exactly what this character captures.

Picture this. The character 雨 is a pictograph, meaning it started as a little drawing of the thing it names. Look at it closely:

雨

The horizontal bar at the top is the sky. The box just below it is a cloud. And those four small dots inside? They are falling raindrops. Ancient scribes drew this thousands of years ago, and the modern character still keeps that same shape — sky, cloud, drops.

Simple rule. 雨 (yǔ, second tone) means rain — the weather event itself. It also acts as a radical (a building-block element) inside many other characters related to water falling from the sky: snow (雪), fog (雾), thunder (雷). Whenever you see 雨 sitting at the top of a character, there is a good chance the meaning involves weather or something coming down from the clouds.

Try it yourself. Look at the four dots in 雨 and count them — you will always find exactly four. Now trace the character with your finger: sky-bar, cloud-box, four drops. Can you see the rainstorm hiding inside those strokes? Once you do, you will never forget what 雨 means.
