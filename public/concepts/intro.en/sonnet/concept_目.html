🌱 目

Have you ever squinted at something far away, trying to get a better look? That simple act — the eye working to see — is exactly what the character 目 captures.

Picture this. Draw an oval for an eye. Inside it, add two horizontal lines to represent the pupil and the eyelid folds. Now tilt the whole thing upright. That is essentially 目 (mù). It is one of the oldest Chinese characters, a pictograph — a word that means "a drawing that became a symbol." You are not memorizing an abstract squiggle; you are reading a tiny sketch of an eye.

Simple rule. 目 (mù) means eye, and by extension, to see or to look. Whenever you spot 目 hiding inside a more complex character, there is almost always something visual going on — seeing, watching, gazing, or appearance. Think of it as a built-in visual clue, like a label that whispers, "something about eyes or sight lives here."

Try it yourself. Look at the character 看 (kàn), which means to look or to watch. Notice the bottom half — that is 目 right there. The top part shows a hand shading the eyes, like someone peering into the distance. Put them together and the meaning arrives naturally: a hand over an eye, looking out. Once you see 目, you cannot unsee it.
