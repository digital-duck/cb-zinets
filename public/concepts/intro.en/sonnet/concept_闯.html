🍃 闯

Have you ever watched a horse burst through a gate — not waiting, not asking, just charging forward with unstoppable energy? That single vivid image is the heart of today's character.

Picture this. Look closely at 闯: you will see 门 (mén), the character for gate or door, framing the outer shape. You already met 门 when you learned 南 — it is the same open frame, the same sense of a threshold between here and there. Tucked inside is 马 (mǎ), the character for horse. A horse. Inside a gate. Mid-charge. The whole character is a frozen moment of bold, muscular momentum.

Simple rule. 闯 (chuǎng) means to rush into, to push through, or to venture boldly. It carries the sense of breaking through barriers — sometimes literally, sometimes metaphorically. In the idiom 走南闯北 (zǒu nán chuǎng běi, "travel south and push through the north"), 闯 describes the gritty, adventurous spirit of someone who doesn't just go somewhere — they forge their way there. 走 is the calm step; 闯 is the brave charge.

Try it yourself. Think of a time you pushed into unfamiliar territory — a new school, a new city, a new skill. That nervous-but-determined feeling? That is 闯 energy. Write the character slowly: draw 门 first, then add 马 inside. The one new piece today is 马 — let that horse remind you that 闯 is always about motion, courage, and crossing a threshold you have never crossed before.
