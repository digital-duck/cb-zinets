🍃 乘

Let’s imagine you're going on a trip. You hop onto a bus to get to the station. Or maybe you take a train to visit your family. These are simple examples of taking something – in this case, transportation.

Picture this: You’re riding a bicycle. You ride it across the park. The word “ride” describes how you move using the bike. “Take” is similar; it means to use something for a purpose or journey.

The basic rule is: 乘 (chéng) means to take, to travel by, or to utilize something to go somewhere or achieve an action. It’s about using something – a vehicle, a tool, even someone's help – to get you where you need to be.

Try it yourself: Think of your favorite way to travel. Do you take the subway? Do you ride your scooter? The word “乘” is often used when describing these actions. It conveys the idea of movement and utilizing something for transit or a specific task. It's a core concept for understanding how things are put into use, bringing us closer to the ancient Chinese proverb: "Those who came before planted trees; those who come after enjoy their shade." The act of “乘” – taking advantage of what others have provided – is at its heart.
