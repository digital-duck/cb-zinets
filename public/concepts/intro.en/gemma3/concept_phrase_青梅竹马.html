Let’s talk about a really interesting idea – one that pops up often in Chinese culture, though it describes a feeling many people across the world can relate to. It's called “青梅竹马,” and it’s a beautiful way of describing a very specific kind of friendship.

Everyday example: Imagine two kids, growing up together. They play together every day, they help each other with homework, they share secrets – a real, close connection develops over many years.

Picture this: Think about your favorite childhood friend. Someone you’ve known for a long time, someone who feels like family. You've shared countless experiences and built a deep understanding of each other. That feeling is what “青梅竹马” tries to capture.

Simple rule: “青梅竹马” (pronounced "qing mei zhu ma") essentially means “plum buddy” or “pear buddy.” The literal translation comes from an old custom: when young boys and girls would often exchange plums and pears as gifts during the summer, symbolizing a budding relationship and friendship. It’s about two people who have known each other since childhood and share a strong bond built on shared experiences and mutual understanding.
