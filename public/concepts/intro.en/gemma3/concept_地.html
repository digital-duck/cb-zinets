🍃 地

Let's start with something familiar: the ground beneath your feet. You’re sitting on it right now, aren’t you? That solid surface is what we mean when we talk about “地” – literally translated as "earth." But "earth" isn't just dirt; it’s everything that makes up the planet we live on.

Everyday Example: Think about building a sandcastle on the beach. You’re using sand, which is part of the earth. Or consider a tree – its roots are buried deep in the earth, drawing nutrients and water from it. Even mountains are made of earth!

Picture this: Imagine our planet as a giant layered cake. The bottom layer is the core—a super-hot, dense ball of metal. Above that is the mantle, a thick layer of mostly solid rock. Then comes the crust – the outermost shell where we live, and it's broken into pieces called tectonic plates.

Simple rule: 地 (the earth) is made up of different layers with varying temperatures and compositions. It's not uniform; it changes dramatically as you go deeper. We can represent this roughly like this:

$$ \text{Core} \rightarrow \text{Mantle} \rightarrow \text{Crust} $$

Try it yourself: Next time you’re outside, take a moment to really look at the ground. What colours do you see? Is it sandy, rocky, or muddy? Notice how different materials are part of this vast, complex “地.” It's a fundamental concept – the very foundation of our world!
