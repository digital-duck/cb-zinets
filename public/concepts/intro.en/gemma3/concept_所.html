🍃 所

Let's talk about “所”. It’s a really common little word in Chinese, and it can seem tricky at first, but once you understand its basic job, it becomes much easier to use.

Everyday Example: Imagine your friend Sarah says, "我买了一本书。" (Wǒ mǎi le yī běn shū.) This translates to “I bought a book.” The word “所” is hidden here, but it’s crucial! Let’s break it down.

Picture this: Think of “所” as a tiny helper that connects something to its owner or source. It shows who is doing the action.

Simple rule: “所” usually appears in sentences where you're saying someone did something, and you want to show who was responsible. It’s like adding a little note: “It was him/her/them.”

Try it yourself: Let's create our own sentence. How about "他写了一封信" (Tā xiě le yī fēng xìn) – “He wrote a letter”? Notice the "所" is missing. Now, let’s add it: "他所写的一封信" (Tā suǒ xiě de yī fēng xìn). See how it clarifies that he wrote the letter? “所” in this case means “his”.

The key takeaway is that "所" provides a quick and easy way to indicate possession or association. We'll explore different uses of “所” in more detail as we continue learning!
