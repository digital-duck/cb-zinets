Let’s talk about “重,” which is a really fundamental concept in Chinese. It's often translated as “heavy” or “weight,” but it actually carries a much broader meaning than just physical heaviness. Think about it – you might say a conversation is “重” (zhòng) if it’s serious and important. Or that a problem is “重” (zhòng) because it's difficult to solve.

Everyday example: Imagine carrying a heavy bag of groceries. You feel the weight of it, right? That feeling of heaviness – that’s one way to understand "重."

Picture this: You’re trying to build a sandcastle on the beach. The tide is coming in and threatens to wash it away. The castle feels “重” (zhòng) because it's fighting against a powerful force.

Simple rule: “重” (zhòng) generally means something has significant impact, importance, or difficulty. It indicates a substantial amount of something – whether that’s physical weight, emotional burden, or intellectual challenge. It can also indicate a return or resurgence, as we'll see later when discussing the phrase "卷土重来" (juǎn tù chóng lái).
