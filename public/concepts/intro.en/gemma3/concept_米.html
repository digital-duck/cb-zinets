🍃 米

🍃 米

Let’s talk about rice – specifically, 米 (pronounced “mee”). You probably eat it every day! But what exactly is it? It's a tiny seed that grows into a huge plant, and we harvest the seeds to make a food we love.

Everyday Example: Think about a bowl of white rice you had for dinner last night. That’s 米. It’s a staple food in many countries, especially across East Asia – places like China, Japan, Korea, and Vietnam.

Picture this: Imagine a field covered in tall green stalks. Each stalk is full of small, hard grains – that's rice plants! Farmers carefully harvest these grains, separating them from the plant.

Simple Rule: Rice itself isn’t particularly tasty on its own. It’s quite bland and chewy. That’s why we cook it. Cooking changes its texture and flavor dramatically.

Try it yourself: Next time you eat rice, notice how different it is before and after cooking. Before, it's hard and dry. After, it's soft, fluffy, and full of starch – a complex carbohydrate that gives us energy. The amount of starch varies slightly depending on the variety of 米 used, but it’s always a key factor in its final texture.
