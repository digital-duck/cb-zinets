🌱 囗

Let’s talk about something really fundamental to how we understand the world: 前因后果 – which literally means “cause and effect.” It's the idea that everything happens because of something else, and that chain of events is what shapes our reality. We often jump straight to ‘why,’ but understanding ‘what came before’ is key.

Everyday Example: Imagine you’re late for work. You might think, “Why am I always late?” But let's break it down. Maybe you overslept because your alarm didn’t go off (the cause). The alarm not going off could be because the batteries died (another cause). And the batteries dying might be due to leaving the charger out of the wall! See? It’s a chain.

Picture this: You're building with LEGO bricks. If you don't put one brick on top of another, it falls down. The falling down is the effect – and the not-putting-it-on is the cause.

Simple rule: Every event has a reason behind it. To figure out 前因后果, we need to ask "What happened before?" repeatedly. It's like tracing back a river’s source.

Try it yourself: Think of something simple that happened today, like you drinking a glass of water. What caused you to drink the water? (Maybe you were thirsty.) Then, what caused that? (Maybe you had a workout). Keep going! You're starting to see the connections.
