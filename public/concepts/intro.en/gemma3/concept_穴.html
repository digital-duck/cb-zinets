🍃 穴

🍃 穴

Let’s talk about something really fundamental in Chinese medicine called “穴” (pronounced “chǔ”). It's a word that sounds mysterious, but it’s actually quite intuitive once you understand what it means.

Everyday Example: Think about a keyhole on a door. You insert the key into a specific space – that space is a little pocket where the key fits perfectly. 穴 are similar; they are small, specific locations within your body.

Picture this: Imagine you’re building with LEGOs. Each brick has a particular shape and fit for another brick. Now imagine tiny “bricks” within your body – these are what we consider 穴. They aren't big openings like wounds, but rather very precise points.

Simple rule: A 穴 is simply a point on the surface of your body (or sometimes inside) where energy flows and can be influenced. It’s a location with a distinct character.

Try it yourself: Let’s start simple. Close your eyes and think about your hand. Now, imagine you're pointing to one specific spot on your palm – maybe the little bump near your thumb. That tiny bump is likely a 穴. We can call it the “acupressure point” for that location on your hand.

In Chinese medicine, these 穴 are linked to pathways called meridians (经络), which we’ll explore in more detail next. Understanding 穴 is the first step towards understanding how this system works!
