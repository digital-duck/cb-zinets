🍃 四

Let’s talk about “四,” which in Chinese means “four.” It's a really fundamental number, and you see it everywhere in China – from counting objects to representing important concepts.

Everyday example: Think about your hands. You have four fingers on each hand! That's why the number four is so familiar. You also might count four cups of tea while you’re having a break, or see four people sitting around a table.

Picture this: Imagine building with blocks. If you stack four red blocks on top of three blue blocks, you have a small tower representing the number four. This isn't just about counting; it's about quantity.

Simple rule: The Chinese character for “four” is 四 (pronounced "sì"). It looks like a square with a horizontal line through it. This shape itself can represent things arranged in rows and columns – think of a chessboard, which has four squares across and eight down.

Try it yourself: Let’s say you have four apples. If someone gives you two more, how many apples do you have now? Write the number out: Four plus two equals...? The answer is six! This simple addition exercise helps you understand how “four” fits into a bigger system of numbers. 四 is a building block for understanding much larger quantities too.
