🌱 东

Let’s talk about “东,” which is one of the most fundamental directions in Chinese. It's a word that describes where something is or moves towards. Think about it like this: you might say your house is "to the east" of your friend's house. That means your house is on the opposite side of your friend’s from where the sun rises.

Everyday example: Imagine you’re playing a game and need to move your piece. You might tell someone, “Move your piece 东!” – that simply means "move it eastward."

Picture this: You're standing in a field, looking at a sunrise. The sun is rising in the east, right? Everything to your right as you face the sun is considered “东.”

Simple rule: “东” always represents the direction of the rising sun. It’s a basic reference point for navigating and describing locations. We can represent this direction mathematically using an angle: $0^\circ$. This means it's exactly 0 degrees, like a perfectly straight line pointing towards the east.

Try it yourself: Close your eyes and imagine you're walking eastward from where you are now. How far do you think you’d walk before you reached a large building? Consider that direction – 东 – is used constantly in maps, directions, and everyday conversations.
