🍃 脚

Let’s start with a really common thing: your feet! Think about walking – what do you use to walk? Your feet! They're the part of your body that connects you to the ground. We use the word “脚” (pronounced ‘jiao’) in Chinese to refer specifically to feet. It’s a core part of our bodies, and it’s used all the time when we talk about movement and balance.

Picture this: You're rushing to catch a bus. You’re trying to put one foot in front of the other, quickly and efficiently. That action – putting one foot forward – is fundamental to how we move. “脚” (jiao) literally means "foot." It’s one character, but it represents a surprisingly complex system!

Now, let's break down a simple rule: The word “脚” describes the lower part of your leg that you use for walking and standing. It’s distinct from your thigh or your shin. We often use it to describe actions related to movement downward – like stepping down stairs, or kicking something.

Try it yourself: When someone says they are "忙脚乱" (busy and confused), they're talking about a situation where their feet are moving around quickly and without purpose. This is because “脚” relates directly to physical movement and instability - a feeling of being unsettled. Think about how you feel when you’re running late – that feeling of urgency often translates into a sensation of having "忙脚乱" (busy feet).
