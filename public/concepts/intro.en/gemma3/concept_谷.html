🍃 谷

Let’s start with a simple thing: rice. Think about your favorite meal – maybe it has rice. Rice is a staple food for many people around the world, and it's a great example of what we mean by “谷” (pronounced "gu" in Chinese, meaning ‘grain’).

Everyday Example: Imagine you’re making a simple dinner. You might have steamed rice alongside some vegetables and meat. That rice is a key part of your meal – it’s a food source!

Picture this: Now picture a vast field stretching out before you, covered in stalks of wheat or barley. Farmers are carefully harvesting these plants, collecting the seeds inside. These seeds are also considered 谷.

Simple Rule: “谷” (gu) refers to any kind of grain – things like rice, wheat, corn, barley, oats… basically, cereal crops that we grow for food. It’s a category for all these harvested seeds. It's important to note that "grain" itself is a broad term referring to the edible seeds of various plants.

Try it yourself: Let’s say you eat oatmeal in the morning. Oatmeal is made from ground-up oats, which are a type of 谷. So, even your breakfast can be part of understanding this concept! The core idea is about harvested seeds that we use as food.
