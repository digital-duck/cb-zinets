🌸 Phrase 口是心非

Let’s explore something fascinating – a concept called “口是心非” (kǒu shì xīn fēi). It doesn't have one exact English translation, but it describes how our words and feelings can sometimes be different.

Everyday Example: Imagine you’re telling your family you love their new house. You might say, “It’s beautiful!” even if secretly you find a few things that bother you. Or perhaps you tell someone, "I'm not worried about the test," when really you are feeling anxious.

Picture this: Think of a child who says they don’t want dessert, but then quickly grabs a second helping! It’s the difference between what we say and how we truly feel inside.

Simple Rule: “口是心非” means that your words don't always match your real feelings or intentions. It’s when there's a gap between what you express outwardly and what’s actually going on in your mind. This doesn’t mean you are lying; it simply reflects complicated emotions we might not have fully explained.

Try it yourself: Think about the last time you spoke to someone. Were your words exactly how you felt? Sometimes, our feelings can be hidden behind what we say. Recognizing these moments helps us understand this common human experience better.
