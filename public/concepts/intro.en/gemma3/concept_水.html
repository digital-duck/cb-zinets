🌱 水

Let’s start with something incredibly familiar: water. Think about it – you drink it, take showers with it, plants need it to grow, and even rain comes from it! Water is everywhere around us, and it's a fundamental part of our world.

Everyday Example: Imagine you’re thirsty after playing sports. You reach for a glass of water, right? That simple act relies on the existence of this substance we call water.

Picture This: Now picture a flowing river. See how the water constantly moves and changes shape? Or consider a still pond – the surface reflects the sky above, showing us the calm nature of the water beneath. Water can be dynamic or peaceful; it depends on many things.

Simple Rule: We define “water” as a substance that’s generally liquid at room temperature (that's what makes it useful for drinking and washing!). It’s made up of molecules – tiny, tiny particles - that are attracted to each other, which is why it flows and takes the shape of its container. These attractions we call "forces."

Try it yourself: Take a small glass of water. Observe how it fills the glass, taking its form. Notice how you can pour it out, changing its shape again. That’s water in action! This simple observation is a key first step in understanding this essential element.
