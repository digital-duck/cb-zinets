🌱 反

Let’s start with a simple example: You’re baking cookies. You follow the recipe exactly – you add flour, sugar, butter, and eggs. But when you put them in the oven, poof! They burn to a crisp. What happened? You did something wrong, right? Maybe you left the oven on too high, or maybe you didn’t check them often enough.

Picture this: You see a pattern of raindrops falling – down, down, down. Then suddenly, the wind picks up and pushes all the raindrops up, back towards the clouds. That's reversing the original direction.

The word "反" (fǎn) in Chinese roughly means “opposite” or “reverse”. It’s used when something is done in a way that goes against what was expected or normal. Think about it like turning something around – you might flip a coin to get heads, but if you want tails, you're doing the opposite.

Simple rule: "反" describes an action or situation that moves against a previous state or direction. It’s about change and opposition.

Try it yourself: Imagine a river flowing downstream. What happens when there’s a dam built across it? The water doesn't just keep flowing down; it flows upwards, against the natural flow. That’s "反" in action!
