Let’s talk about having a good feeling about something – not just guessing, but actually sensing that it might be true. In Chinese, there's a wonderful phrase called “有鼻子有眼” (yǒu lǐ zǐ yǒu yǎn). Let’s break down what this means step by step.

Everyday Example: Imagine you're at a street fair and someone offers you a piece of fruit. You don't know the vendor, but something about their smile, the way they present the fruit, or even just the smell – all combine to make you instinctively feel that this is good quality fruit. Simple Rule: “有鼻子有眼” literally translates to "having nose and eyes." It describes a situation where your senses – primarily sight and smell (hence the ‘nose’) – combined with a gut feeling or intuition, lead you to believe something is likely true or of good quality. It’s about building a case based on multiple pieces of information that subtly suggest a positive outcome.

Try it Yourself: Think about buying a used car.
