🌱 口

Let’s talk about “口” – which literally translates to “mouth” in Chinese. Now, before you imagine someone saying "hello," let's think about it a little differently. We use the word “mouth” all the time when we eat! You put food into your mouth with your mouth, and then you chew it and swallow it. It’s how we get nourishment from the world around us.

Picture this: you’re enjoying a delicious apple pie. You take a bite, and the sweet, warm filling goes directly into your mouth. That's the function of your mouth – to receive things like food and drink.

The simple rule is this: “口” represents an input. It's where something enters a system or process. Think about it like a doorway; your mouth is the doorway for everything you consume. It’s the starting point for many transformations within us.

Try it yourself! Close your eyes and imagine taking a sip of water. Where does that water go? It goes into your mouth, right? That's “口” in action – accepting something new. This single concept, "mouth" as an input, is the foundation for understanding more complex ideas about how things change and interact.
