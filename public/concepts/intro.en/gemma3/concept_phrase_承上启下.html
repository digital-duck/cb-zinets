🌸 Phrase 承上启下

Let’s imagine you're building with LEGO bricks. You might start with a small baseplate – that’s your “上” (shang) or “previous” part. You build something on top of it: a little house, maybe. That’s the “下” (xia) or “next” part. “承上启下” literally translates to "carry what is above, open up what is below". It’s a really useful phrase in Chinese that describes how things should connect and flow together.

Picture this: you're writing a story. The first chapter sets the scene – that’s your “承上” (carry what is previous). The next chapters build on that, adding characters and plot points – that’s your “启下” (open up what is below). You aren’t just randomly throwing ideas in; each new part needs to relate back to the earlier parts.

The simple rule is: every idea or step should be connected to what came before and provide a foundation for what comes after. Think of it like building with blocks – you need a solid base to build taller structures. It's about creating a chain of cause and effect, where one thing leads logically to the next.

Try it yourself: Imagine you’re explaining how to bake a cake. You start with gathering ingredients (“承上”) – flour, sugar, eggs. Then you mix them together (“启下”) – preparing for adding baking powder and finally, baking the cake. See? Each step builds on the last, setting up the final delicious result!
