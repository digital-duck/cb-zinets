🌸 Phrase 七上八下

Let’s explore a common Japanese phrase: 七上八下 (shichi jō hachi hakase). It might seem tricky, but it describes a pattern you see all around you – like how things grow and change!

Everyday Example: Have you ever watched a plant grow? It starts small, then gets bigger as it receives sunlight and water. Then, it grows even larger before eventually returning to a smaller size.

Picture this: Imagine building with blocks. You begin with a few blocks (shichi – meaning seven), adding more until your tower is tall (jō – meaning up). Next, you slowly take some blocks away (hachi – meaning eight) until it’s shorter, and finally, you knock the whole thing down (hakase - meaning down).

Simple rule: 七上八下 means a cycle of getting bigger and then getting smaller. It's like watching a wave move: rising up, reaching its highest point, and then falling back down. The numbers are just a helpful way to remember this pattern! It’s a way of thinking about change.

Try it yourself: Look around you! Think about the seasons – Spring brings growth (jō), Summer sees even more growth (hachi), Autumn is when things start to shrink (hakase) and finally, Winter brings a return to a smaller size. Can you see this pattern happening in other things too?
