Let's talk about a really interesting idea called “前功尽弃” – which roughly translates to “all prior effort is lost.” It sounds bleak, doesn’t it? But it describes a surprisingly common experience in many areas of life, from sports to business and even personal relationships. We’re going to break this down into smaller pieces so you can really understand it.

Everyday Example: Imagine you’re preparing for a marathon. You spend months training, running miles every week, following a strict diet…and then, on race day, something just goes wrong – maybe you get a stomach ache, or the weather is terrible. All that hard work suddenly feels useless. It's like all those previous efforts – your training, your dedication – vanished completely.

Picture this: You’ve built a beautiful sandcastle on the beach. You’ve spent hours carefully shaping it, adding towers and moats, feeling incredibly proud of your creation. Then, a big wave comes along and washes it away completely. It's devastating!

Simple rule: “前功尽弃” means that sometimes, despite all your previous work, things can change so dramatically that what you’ve done before no longer matters or is even helpful. It doesn't mean your effort was wasted entirely – it meant the conditions have shifted. It highlights how unstable situations often are.

Try it yourself: Think about a project at work. You might have spent weeks planning and researching, building a detailed roadmap. But then a key client changes their requirements suddenly, rendering your entire plan obsolete. That feeling of having to start almost from scratch? That’s “前功尽弃” in action. It's a reminder that adaptability is crucial.
