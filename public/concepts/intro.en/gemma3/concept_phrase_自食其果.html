🌸 Phrase 自食其果

Let’s talk about cause and effect. Have you ever done something you knew was a bad idea, like eating an entire cake after promising yourself you wouldn't? And then you felt awful afterward – sluggish, maybe even with a stomachache? That feeling is the result of your action. It’s what happens because you did that thing.

Picture this: You plant a small seed in your garden. You don’t water it or give it sunlight. What do you think will happen to that seed? Probably nothing good, right? It won't grow into a beautiful flower or vegetable. The lack of care – the cause – leads to a negative effect.

Simple rule: Every action has a consequence. Think of it like this: if you push a domino (the cause), it will knock over other dominoes (the effects). It’s not just one domino falling; it's a chain reaction. The key is understanding that the effect is directly linked to what you did.

Try it yourself: Let’s say you decide to stay up late playing video games instead of studying for an important test. What might be the effect of that choice? (Pause and think about it.) You could get a bad grade on the test, or feel stressed and unprepared. See how the action – staying up late – led directly to those consequences. That’s the essence of “自食其果” – you eat what you sow.
