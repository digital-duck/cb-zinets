🍃 易

Let’s talk about something incredibly fundamental: yi. In Chinese, “易” (pronounced approximately ‘ee’) is a word that packs a lot of meaning, and it's one we’ll be returning to often. It doesn't have a single, perfect English translation, but at its core, it describes something that is easy, simple, or natural. Think about it – you probably use the word "easy" all the time!

Everyday Example: Imagine trying to open a jar of pickles. If the lid twists easily, it’s yi – simple and straightforward. But if you have to force it with all your might, it's not!

Picture this: You’re learning to ride a bike. At first, it feels incredibly difficult, right? You wobble, you fall, you struggle. But as you practice, it becomes yi – almost automatic.

Simple rule: “易” describes things that require little effort or skill. It’s about a natural flow, a comfortable fit. It suggests something isn't complicated or forced.

Try it yourself: Think of your favorite hobby. What makes it enjoyable? Is it because the skills involved are yi, or do you find it challenging and difficult? Consider the difference between baking a simple cookie (which should be yi) and building a complex computer program – those are vastly different levels of difficulty. "易" is about recognizing that naturalness in whatever you’re doing.
