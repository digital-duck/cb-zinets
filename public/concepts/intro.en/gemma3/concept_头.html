🍃 头

Let’s start with something very familiar: your head! Think about it – you use your head to think, to feel, to see the world around you. In Chinese, “头” (tóu) is a really important word. It doesn't just mean "head" in the physical sense; it carries a lot of meaning beyond that.

Everyday Example: Imagine someone says, “他很聪明” (Tā hěn cōngmíng) – “He’s very smart.” The word “头” is actually hidden within “聪明” (cōngmíng). “聪明” literally means "head-smart," implying cleverness and intelligence. It's a beautiful example of how the same character can represent completely different ideas depending on the context.

Picture this: You’re building with LEGO bricks. The first brick you put down is your “头” – the starting point for your creation. It sets everything else in motion.

Simple rule: “头” (tóu) often represents the beginning, the primary element, or the core of something. It can be a place, an idea, or even a person's character.

Try it yourself: Let’s say you want to describe a strong leader. You might say “他有很棒的头” (Tā yǒu hěn bàng de tóu) – "He has a great head" – meaning he’s a wise and decisive person. Notice how we're still using “头” in a metaphorical way! It’s all about understanding the core concept behind this versatile character.
