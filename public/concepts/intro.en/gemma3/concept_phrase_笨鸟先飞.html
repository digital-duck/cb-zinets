🌸 Phrase 笨鸟先飞

Let’s talk about a phrase that sounds a little bit like an animal saying! It's called “笨鸟先飞” (bèn niǎo xiān fēi). It literally translates to "foolish birds fly first." Don’t worry, we aren’t talking about actual silly birds. This phrase is used as encouragement – it means that sometimes you need to take the initial step, even if it seems a little awkward or difficult.

Picture this: You want to learn how to ride a bike. The very first thing you have to do is just try to pedal. You’re likely going to wobble, maybe fall down a bit, and feel a little silly. That feeling of being clumsy? That's part of the process! "笨鸟先飞" encourages you to start anyway – to not be afraid of making mistakes or looking foolish in the beginning.

Simple rule: The phrase suggests that taking action, even imperfectly, is better than waiting for everything to feel perfect before starting at all. It’s about overcoming hesitation and initiating something.

Try it yourself: Think of a skill you've always wanted to learn – maybe playing an instrument or speaking a new language. Instead of worrying about being bad at first, just start! Do one small thing today related to that skill. Even if it feels uncomfortable, remember “笨鸟先飞” and keep going. The key is starting.
