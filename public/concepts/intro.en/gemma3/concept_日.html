🌱 日

🌱 日

Let’s start with something incredibly familiar: the sun! You see it every day, right? It rises in the east and sets in the west. That daily movement is what we call “日” – pronounced "jee" – and it's a fundamental way we measure time.

Everyday Example: Think about waking up each morning. The sun comes up, signaling that it’s a new day. You might say, "It’s daytime!" because the sun is shining.

Picture this: Imagine a giant clock face. Instead of numbers 1 through 12, we mark it with images of the sun at different points in its journey across the sky. These images represent specific times of the day.

Simple rule: “日” refers to the cycle the sun completes as it moves across the sky. It’s a way to divide our days into periods – morning, afternoon, evening, and night. A day is simply one complete cycle of the sun. We use this concept to say things like "It's 9 am" or "It's sunset".

Try it yourself: Look out your window right now. What time of day is it? Is it daytime (when the sun is shining)? Or is it nighttime (when the sun is hidden)? Notice how the movement of the “日” – the sun – dictates our understanding of time.
