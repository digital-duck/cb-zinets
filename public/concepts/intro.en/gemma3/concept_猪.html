Let’s start with a simple animal: the pig. You've probably seen them – they’re often kept on farms to raise meat and produce food like bacon and ham. But what is a pig?

Picture this: you’re at a market, and you see a group of animals milling around. Some are sleek and fast, others are slower and bulkier. A pig is one of those bulky animals. They're known for their stout bodies and short legs.

A pig (pronounced “pee”) is an animal belonging to the family Suidae. This means it’s closely related to other animals like wild boars and domestic hogs. Pigs are generally characterized by their rounded shape, strong snouts used for digging, and surprisingly intelligent behavior. They're excellent at learning tricks and problem-solving – they can even be trained!

Simple rule: A pig is a large, typically gray or pink animal with a distinctive snout and sturdy build. Think of it as a creature designed for foraging on the ground, searching for roots, tubers, and insects to eat.

Try it yourself: Close your eyes and imagine an animal that’s good at digging. What does it look like? Chances are, you're picturing a pig! In fact, scientists believe pigs have excellent spatial memory, much better than many other mammals.
