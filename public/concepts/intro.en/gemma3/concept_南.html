🍃 南

Let's talk about “南,” one of the most fundamental directions in Chinese culture. It’s a word that describes something very specific: south. But it's much more than just a compass point; it represents ideas about travel, experience, and even values.

Everyday Example: Imagine you're planning a trip. You might say, "I want to go south for the winter." This simply means you’re choosing a location further down on Earth relative to where you are currently standing – that’s what “south” represents geographically.

Picture this: Think about a traditional Chinese painting of mountains and rivers. The artist will often position the tallest, most impressive peaks towards the south. Why? Because in Chinese culture, the south is considered the warmer, more auspicious direction.

Simple Rule: "南" (nán) always means south. It’s opposite to “北” (běi), which means north. Think of it like this: if you're facing north, south is to your right.

Try it yourself: Close your eyes and point towards the south. Now, say "南" out loud. You're starting to understand a core part of Chinese thinking about space and direction! It’s a simple word with profound cultural weight.
