Let’s start with something familiar: you! You are a person. But what does “person” really mean? Simply put, a person is an individual being – someone who thinks, feels, and acts. It's the label we give to ourselves and others when we recognize that they have their own unique experiences and abilities.

Picture this: you’re deciding whether or not to eat an apple. You consider if you want an apple, if you like apples, and if you have time to enjoy it. All of those thoughts and feelings are part of what makes you a person.

The simple rule is this: A person is defined by their capacity for awareness and action. It’s not just about breathing or existing; it's about being aware that you exist, and having the ability to make choices – even small ones like choosing which apple to eat! We use the word “person” a lot because it’s a convenient way to talk about other individuals with similar qualities.

Try it yourself: Think of someone you know well - maybe a friend or family member. What makes them a person? Is it their personality, their dreams, or their ability to care for others? There isn't one single answer; being a person is really complex and beautiful!
