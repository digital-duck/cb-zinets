🍃 能

Let’s talk about “能,” which is one of the core ideas behind 熟能生巧 – a really important Chinese concept. It’s often translated as "ability" or “capacity,” but it's actually much more than just being able to do something. Think about learning to ride a bicycle. At first, you wobble, you fall, and you feel completely frustrated. You might not think you can do it.

Everyday Example: Imagine trying to bake your first cake. The recipe seems simple enough – flour, sugar, eggs – but when you mix them, it looks…wrong! It’s sticky, lumpy, and doesn't resemble the picture on the box. You might feel like you can’t make a good cake.

Picture this: Now imagine that same person baking a cake ten years later. They instinctively know how much flour to use, how long to bake it for, and even add a secret ingredient without thinking. It comes out perfect every time!

Simple rule: “能” refers to the potential to do something well – the capacity that develops over time with practice. It’s not just about knowing how initially; it's about your body and mind learning how to perform a task efficiently. It represents the growing confidence and skill you gain as you repeat an action.

Try it yourself: Choose a simple activity, like tying your shoes or drawing a basic shape. The first time is probably clumsy and slow. But if you practice regularly – even just for five minutes each day – you’ll find that "能" – your ability to do it smoothly and accurately – will steadily increase.
