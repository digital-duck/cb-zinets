🌸 Phrase 承前启后

Let’s talk about a really important idea in Chinese philosophy and writing – it’s called “承前启后,” which we can translate literally as “carry forward, open to behind.” Don't worry if that sounds complicated! It’s actually quite simple.

Imagine you're building with LEGO bricks. You wouldn't just start stacking them randomly, would you? You build upon what you’ve already built. You might use a baseplate (that’s like the “前,” or ‘front’ – what came before) and then add more blocks to create a tower. You are carrying forward your previous work.

Now, picture this: you're writing a story. You start with an introduction—setting the scene and introducing characters. Then, you continue building on that introduction, developing the plot and exploring themes. But what happens at the end of the story? You don’t just abruptly stop! You open to behind – you provide a resolution, tying up loose ends and leaving the reader with a sense of completion.

The simple rule is this: When creating anything – whether it's a piece of writing, a plan, or even a LEGO creation – always consider what has come before and how your work connects to what will come later. It’s about continuity and building towards a complete whole. 承前启后 reminds us that everything is interconnected.

Try it yourself: Think about a task you're working on. Before starting, ask yourself, “What did I do last time? And how does this new step connect to the overall goal?” You’ll find it helps you stay focused and create something truly meaningful.
