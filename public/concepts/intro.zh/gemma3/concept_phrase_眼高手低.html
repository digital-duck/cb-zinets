🌸 Phrase 眼高手低

Let’s talk about a phrase that's really common in China – “眼高手低” (yǎn gāo tú dī). It sounds a little complicated at first, but it actually describes something we all experience to some degree.

Everyday Example: Imagine your friend wants to learn calligraphy. They see beautiful, professional-looking characters online and immediately want to copy them exactly, without understanding the basic strokes or techniques involved. They spend hours trying to reproduce these complex images, but their attempts always look messy and uneven.

Picture this: You’re watching a chef make a complicated dish on television – maybe a layered dessert with intricate decorations. The chef seems effortlessly skilled, executing each step perfectly. But when you try to recreate the recipe yourself, following the instructions exactly, it doesn't turn out as beautifully because you haven’t grasped the underlying principles of how the different flavors and textures interact.

Simple rule: “眼高手低” (yǎn gāo tú dī) literally means "high eyes, low skills.” It describes someone who has a lot of ideas about what something should look like or how it should be done – they have high expectations – but lacks the necessary skills to actually achieve that. It’s like wanting to build a skyscraper without knowing anything about architecture or engineering.

Try it yourself: Think about any hobby you've tried to learn, like playing an instrument or learning a new language. Have you ever been frustrated because you were trying to do something impressive before you had the basic skills down? That feeling of frustration is often linked to “眼高手低.” It’s a reminder that progress usually comes from building a solid foundation first!
