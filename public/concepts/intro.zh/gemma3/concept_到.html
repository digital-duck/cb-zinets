🍃 到

Let’s talk about “到” – it's a really common word in Chinese that can feel a little tricky at first. It doesn’t always translate directly to one English word.

Everyday Example: Imagine you are waiting for your friend, 小红 (Xiǎo Hóng). You say, "她什么时候到？" (Tā shénme shíhòu dào?) – “When will she arrive?” You're asking about her arrival time.

Picture this: Think of a journey. You’re traveling to your grandmother’s house. As you get closer, you are "到家" (dàojiā) – meaning you have arrived home. It represents reaching a destination.

Simple Rule: “到” generally means “to arrive,” “to reach,” or “to complete.” But crucially, it often signifies completion linked to a specific point in time or space. It’s about getting somewhere.

Try it yourself: Let’s say you finish your homework. You could say 你把作业做到完了 (Nǐ bǎ zuòyè zuò dào wán le) – “You finished your homework.” "到" here indicates the completion of the task. Notice how it's not just ‘finished’, but 'finished to something'.

The key is to connect it with a place or an action that has a clear endpoint. It’s a really important word for understanding movement and accomplishment in Chinese!
