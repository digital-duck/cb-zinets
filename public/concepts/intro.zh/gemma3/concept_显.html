Let’s start with something really basic: noticing things. Think about when you're walking down the street, and suddenly you see a bright red umbrella – completely out of place on a sunny day. You notice it because it stands out. That’s where “显” (xiǎn) comes in.

Everyday Example: Imagine you’re baking cookies. You add chocolate chips to the dough. At first, you can't really see them mixed in; they’re hidden. But as you mix and knead, the chocolate chips start to become more visible. They begin to show themselves.

Picture this: You’re looking for a lost earring. It’s dark, and the floor is covered with carpet. You wouldn't find it just by randomly searching; you need something – perhaps a flashlight – to make it appear or become visible.

Simple Rule: “显” (xiǎn) means “to appear,” “to show itself,” or "to become visible.” It’s about something moving from being hidden to being noticed. It's the opposite of something being concealed, or kept secret.

Try it yourself: Close your eyes and think about a colour – let’s say blue. Now, imagine you’re looking at a blue shirt. At first, all you know is that it exists. But when you look at it directly, the blue color starts to show itself to your eyes. That's “显” in action! It's about making something apparent.
