🍃 油

Let's talk about “油,” which in Chinese means “oil.” It’s a really common word, and it describes something quite familiar: think about cooking. When you stir-fry vegetables in a wok, what are you using? That’s right – oil! But "油" is much more than just cooking oil. It represents a fascinating idea that applies to lots of different situations.

Everyday example: Imagine a small flame flickering on a campfire. You add another piece of wood, and the flame grows bigger and brighter. Then you add another piece of wood – and suddenly it's roaring! This rapid increase in size is often caused by adding more fuel – which, in this case, could be described as “油.”

Picture this: Let’s say you’re building a sandcastle on the beach. You start with a small pile of sand, and then you add more and more sand to make it bigger. If you keep adding more, the whole castle can collapse! This is similar to what "油" represents.

Simple rule: “油” describes something that gets bigger because you’re adding more of itself. It's like a snowball rolling down a hill – the more snow it picks up, the larger it becomes. We use the term to describe situations where an initial small increase leads to a rapidly accelerating growth.

Try it yourself: Think about your phone battery. When you start using apps, the battery drains quickly. That drain is like "油" – the more you use it (adding electricity), the faster it goes down. It’s not just one charge; it's a continuous process of adding to the problem.
