🍃 思

Let’s talk about “思” (pronounced “sī”). It's a really important word in Chinese, and it’s one of the building blocks for understanding how we think about things. You probably use this word all the time without even realizing it!

Everyday Example: Imagine you’re trying to remember where you put your keys. You might be thinking, “I need to think about it… did I leave them on the table? Or maybe in my bag?” That little act of searching and remembering – that's using “思” in a small way.

Picture this: Imagine you’re planning a birthday party for your friend. You have to consider everything: who to invite, what food to serve, what activities to do. All these decisions and considerations are driven by "思".

Simple rule: “思” basically means “to think,” but it goes beyond just having thoughts. It's about actively considering something, weighing options, or trying to understand a problem. It’s the process of mental effort. We can represent this in a simple way: $思 = 思考 (sī = sīkǎo)$ – “思” is equivalent to "thinking" in Chinese.

Try it yourself: Let's say you’re looking at a beautiful sunset. Instead of just seeing it, try to really think about it. What colors are you seeing? How does the light feel on your skin? What emotions does it evoke in you? That conscious effort – that’s “思” in action.
