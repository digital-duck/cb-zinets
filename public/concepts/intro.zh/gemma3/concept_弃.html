Let’s talk about something called “弃” (qì). It sounds a little mysterious, doesn't it? Don't worry; we'll break it down step-by-step. The word "弃" literally means to discard, to give up, or to abandon. Think of throwing away an old toy you no longer play with – that’s “弃.”

Everyday Example: Imagine you're building a sandcastle on the beach. You spend ages carefully shaping it, adding towers and walls. But then the tide comes in, and suddenly your beautiful castle is being washed away. You might feel disappointed, but you could also 弃 (qì) – you could decide to stop trying to rebuild it and instead enjoy watching the waves.

Picture this: You’re training for a marathon. You've been running diligently for months, following a strict schedule. However, suddenly you develop an injury. Continuing to train would only make things worse. To protect your health and achieve your long-term goal, you have to 弃 (qì) – you need to stop pushing yourself and focus on recovery.

Simple rule: “弃” represents making a conscious decision to relinquish something—a plan, an effort, or even an expectation. It’s not about failure; it's often about recognizing when something isn’t working and choosing a new path. It signifies letting go.

Try it yourself: Think of a small task you’ve been putting off – maybe cleaning your room, or responding to an email. Recognize that continuing to postpone it is causing you stress. Decide 弃 (qì) - accept you can't complete it perfectly right now and schedule a simpler, smaller version for later. That’s the power of “弃.”
