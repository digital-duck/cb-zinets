🍃 功

Let’s start with a simple example: think about helping your younger sibling with their homework. You spend an hour patiently explaining fractions, guiding them through problems, and offering encouragement. That effort – that time you invested in their learning – has a certain kind of value, doesn't it? We call this value “功” (gōng).

Picture this: you’re preparing for a big exam. You spend weeks studying, reviewing notes, doing practice questions, and sacrificing your evenings to learn new concepts. All that work isn't just about getting a good grade; it builds something within you – knowledge, skill, confidence. This accumulated preparation is also "功".

Now, let’s define a key term: 功 (gōng) literally means “achievement” or “effort.” It represents the result of your work, the gains you make through action and exertion. It’s not just about reaching a final destination; it's about the journey itself and what that journey creates within you. Think of it as building up something – like a muscle – with consistent effort.

Simple rule: 功 is created when you put in effort to achieve something. It’s the accumulation of your efforts over time. It's not just one single action, but a series of actions working together.

Try it yourself: Let’s say you decide to learn how to play the guitar. You spend 30 minutes each day practicing chords and scales. That consistent effort – those 30 minutes – is building your "功" in playing the guitar. The more you practice, the more “功” you accumulate, and eventually, you’ll become better at it!
