🌸 Phrase 布衣之交

Let’s talk about a saying called “布衣之交” (bù yī zhī jiāo). It sounds a bit complicated at first, but it's actually quite simple to understand.

Imagine this: You and your best friend both wear plain cotton clothes – what we call "布" (bù) which means cotton fabric - every day. This is the basic meaning behind the phrase. It’s all about shared experiences and a bond formed through common things.

Picture this: Two boys, Li Wei 和 Wang Ming, meet at school wearing simple backpacks and t-shirts. They start talking about their favorite sports, sharing snacks during recess, and studying together for exams. Their friendship grows stronger because they have these shared activities – the '布衣' (bù yī) – that bring them closer.

The simple rule is this: “布衣之交，海灾忘。” (bù yī zhī jiāo, hǎi zāi wàng). This means “Friends made in humble clothing will not forget each other even during a great disaster.” "海灾" (hǎi zāi) refers to a major flood or storm. Essentially, it suggests that friendships built on genuine connection and shared experiences are incredibly strong and resilient – they survive through difficult times.

Try it yourself: Think about people you know who have been friends for a long time. What common activities do they enjoy? What experiences have they shared together? These shared things—like hobbies, interests, or even just everyday moments—are the basis of their friendship, much like that initial connection built on simple clothing. The strength of their bond reflects the depth of those shared experiences.
