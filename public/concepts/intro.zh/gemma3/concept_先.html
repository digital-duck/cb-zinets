🍃 先

Let’s imagine you're building with LEGO bricks. You have a huge box full of different shapes and sizes. Before you start trying to build a spaceship, you might first put down a small baseplate – a flat surface where you can begin. This baseplate is your first step.

Picture this: You want to bake a cake. Do you immediately throw all the ingredients into the mixer? No! First, you measure out the flour, sugar, and eggs. Then you mix them together. The measuring and mixing are your first actions before actually baking.

The word “先” (xiān) in Chinese means "first." It simply indicates that something is done or happens before other things. It’s about order – arranging events or steps so the most important one comes first. Think of it like a race: the runner who starts first has an advantage!

Simple rule: When you're planning something, “先” tells you to think about what needs to happen first. It helps you organize your thoughts and actions.

Try it yourself: Let’s say you want to write a story. What is the first thing you need to do? You need to decide on the main idea of your story! That idea, that initial thought – that's "先." 记住， “先” 代表“首先”，帮助你安排事情的顺序。
