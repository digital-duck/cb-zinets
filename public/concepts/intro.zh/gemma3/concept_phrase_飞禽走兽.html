🌸 Phrase 飞禽走兽

Let’s imagine you're a zoo keeper! You have many different animals – some that fly, and some that walk. You want a simple way to group them together. That’s where “飞禽走兽” comes in.

Picture this: You’re making a list of all the creatures at your zoo. Some are birds, like eagles soaring through the sky, and penguins waddling on the ground. Others are animals that walk, like lions roaming the savanna or elephants lumbering through the jungle.

“飞禽走兽” (fēi qín zǒu shòu) is a Chinese phrase that literally means “flying animals walking animals.” Let’s break it down:

  • 飞 (fēi) – This word means "to fly." Think of an airplane or a bird taking flight.
  • 禽 (qín) – This word refers to birds and other feathered creatures. It's a category for things that can fly.
  • 走 (zǒu) – This word means “to walk” or “to move on foot.”
  • 兽 (shòu) – This word describes animals in general, especially those that don’t fly, like mammals and reptiles.

So, "飞禽走兽" is a way to broadly classify all living things into these three groups: flying creatures, walking creatures, and everything else. It's a simple system for organizing information!
