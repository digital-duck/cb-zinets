Let’s talk about something called “丶” – it sounds a little strange, doesn't it? It’s actually a really useful way to think about how our feelings can sometimes get in the way of being honest with ourselves and others.

Everyday example: Imagine you spend all day working on a beautiful painting for your friend’s birthday. You pour your heart into it, but when you show it to them, instead of saying "I'm so happy you like it!" you burst out with “It’s terrible! I wasted my time!” That feeling—that sudden wave of disappointment and frustration—is what we want to understand.

Picture this: Think of a dog guarding a bone. It’s fiercely protective, right? It might bark and growl even if the person approaching is friendly. Now imagine that dog also has a human heart and mind inside it. That inner conflict – wanting to protect something but also feeling negative emotions – is similar to what “丶” describes.

Simple rule: "丶" means recognizing that your initial reaction to something (like disappointment, anger, or embarrassment) might not be the full story. It’s a reminder that feelings can distort our judgment and lead us to say or do things we later regret. It's about noticing why you feel a certain way before reacting.

Try it yourself: The next time you feel a strong emotion – maybe you receive criticism, or someone cuts you off in traffic – take a deep breath. Ask yourself: "What am I really feeling beneath this anger/frustration/sadness? Is there another possible explanation for what happened?" Just acknowledging the initial feeling is the first step.
