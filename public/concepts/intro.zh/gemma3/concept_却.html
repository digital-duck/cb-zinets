🍃 却

🍃 却

Let’s talk about a little word that packs a surprising punch: 却 (què). It doesn't translate directly into English with one simple word, which is why it can feel a bit tricky at first! Think of it like this: imagine you’re expecting a delicious cake from your friend. You ask them to bake it, and they promise to do so. Then, when you go over, they haven’t made it – that feeling of disappointment mixed with a little understanding is kind of what 却 captures.

Picture this: you tell someone you'll help them move furniture, promising to be there at 9 am sharp. But then, at 9:15 am, you realize you overslept! You text them saying sorry and explaining the situation. The word 却 helps show that even though you messed up (you overslept), there’s still a sense of acknowledging their expectation – you did intend to be there.

The simple rule is this: 却 usually appears when something doesn't match what was expected or promised. It shows a contrast, a little bit of "but..." after an initial statement. It often implies regret or explanation for the difference.

Try it yourself! Let’s say you tell your sibling you’ll wash the dishes after dinner. But then, you get distracted and don't do it. You could say: “我说了我会洗， 却没洗。” (Wǒ shuōle wǒ huì xǐ, què mò xǐ.) – "I said I would wash them, but I didn’t." See how 却 highlights the gap between what you said and what actually happened? It's a useful little word for expressing these kinds of situations in Chinese.
