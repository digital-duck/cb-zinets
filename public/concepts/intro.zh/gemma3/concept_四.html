Let’s talk about the number four – we’ll call it “四” (pronounced “sì”). It's one of the most fundamental numbers in Chinese, and understanding it is a really solid foundation for learning more complex ideas.

Everyday Example: Think about your hands! You have four fingers on each hand. That’s why we use four to represent things like "four friends" or "four legs on a table.” It's a very common thing in our daily lives.

Picture This: Imagine you are building with blocks. You build a wall with four bricks stacked neatly – one, two, three, and then four. Each brick represents a single unit of this number “四”.

Simple Rule: The word "四" itself means simply “four.” We use it to count things up to four. It’s like saying “one, two, three, four!” It's a basic building block for counting.

Try It Yourself: Let’s try counting! Look around you – can you find something that you see exactly four of? Maybe there are four pencils on your desk, or four books in a row. Write down the number "四" and then write down what you found that has four things in it. That's it! You’ve just started to understand this important number.
