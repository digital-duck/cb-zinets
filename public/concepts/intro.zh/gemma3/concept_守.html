You think, "If I just stand here, waiting for the rabbit to come near, it might eventually wander close enough for me to catch it!" The word "守" (shǒu) literally means “to guard,” “to protect,” or “to maintain.” In this context, it refers to a defensive strategy where you don't actively pursue your goal; instead, you focus on securing a fixed point – like Burrow A in our example.
