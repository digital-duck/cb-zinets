🌱 亠口

Let’s talk about a really interesting shape called 亠口 – we’ll say it sounds like “tou-ko.” It's a fundamental building block in Chinese painting and calligraphy, and understanding it is key to appreciating the beauty of these art forms.

Everyday Example: Think about a simple bowl. A lot of bowls have this ╶ shape for their opening – it’s the top part when you’re holding it. This ╶ shape is a very common and recognizable form.

Picture this: Imagine a mountain range. The peak of the mountain, often depicted in Chinese landscape paintings, frequently takes on this 亠口 shape. It represents stability, strength, and reaching for something higher.

Simple rule: 亠口 literally means "top mouth" or “hat-shaped opening.” It describes a rounded, upward-facing form that looks like a small, upright bowl, or perhaps the top of a hat. Notice how it’s defined by its curved edges and the way it points upwards.

Try it yourself: Take a piece of paper and draw a simple triangle. Now, gently curve the top point upwards to create a rounded shape. You've just made a 亠口! Practice drawing several 亠口 shapes in different sizes – you’ll see them appear again and again throughout your study of Chinese art. They are incredibly versatile and used to represent everything from mountains and clouds to people and objects.
