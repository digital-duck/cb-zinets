🌱 东

Let's talk about “东,” which is one of the most fundamental directions in Chinese. It’s a really common word that describes where things are facing or pointing. Think about it like this: when you’re building with LEGO bricks, you might say "put the red brick to the east" – that's using “东” to tell someone which way to put it.

Everyday example: Imagine you’re eating breakfast. You might say, “我朝东吃早饭” (Wǒ cháo dōng zǎo fàn) – “I eat breakfast facing east.” It simply means you are oriented with your face towards the east when having breakfast.

Picture this: The sun rises in the east, doesn’t it? That's why "东" is so strongly connected to the idea of a beginning or a forward direction. It represents a positive movement.

Simple rule: “东” means “east.” It’s one of the four main compass directions – north (北), south (南), west (西), and east (东). They are often used together to describe locations, like "the east side of the park." We can represent these directions using symbols: 东 (dōng) is written as 东方 (dōng fāng) which literally means “eastern direction.”

Try it yourself: Look around you. Can you identify something that faces east? Maybe a window looks out towards the sunrise, or a building has its main entrance facing east. Just think about what "东" represents - a forward-facing direction!
