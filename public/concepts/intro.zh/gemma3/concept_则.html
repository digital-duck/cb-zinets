🍃 则

🍃 则

Let's explore “则” (zé). It’s a key word in Chinese philosophy, especially when we consider learning and wisdom. You might have heard it in the famous saying: “学而不思则罔，思而不学则殆.” Let’s look at this saying closely. It means roughly: "If you learn without thinking, you won't achieve anything; if you think without studying, you could be in trouble."

Everyday Example: Think about learning to ride a bicycle. You can simply follow instructions – someone tells you how to balance and pedal. That’s like “学而不思” – you’re learning how to do it without understanding why it works or what makes you stable.

Picture this: You try riding the bike just following directions, but you wobble and fall over many times. It's hard to get a feel for how your body needs to move!

Simple rule: “则” (zé) connects ideas, showing that one thing leads to another. It’s like saying "then" or "therefore." It suggests a relationship between what you learn and what happens next. In our example, not thinking about balancing (“学而不思”) then causes you to fall.

Try it yourself: Consider learning to play a musical instrument. If you just try to copy the notes without understanding rhythm or music theory – “学而不思” – how will you eventually become a good musician? "则" reminds us that not combining study with thought can lead to difficulties.
