🍃 侯

Let’s imagine you're organizing a school bake sale. You ask your friends to bring cookies, cakes, or brownies – anything sweet! Everyone promises to contribute, but then, a few days before the sale, nobody shows up. This makes you feel frustrated and worried that others won’t follow through on their commitments.

Picture this: You set an expectation – "Everyone will bring something for the bake sale" – and then that expectation isn't met. That feeling of disappointment and concern is what we want to understand today.

The term 侯 (hòu) describes this precise situation. It’s about a potential problem or negative consequence that arises when an expectation isn’t fulfilled. Essentially, it represents the risk of something bad happening if someone doesn't do what they said they would. We can think of it as a ‘warning sign’ before things go wrong.

Simple rule: 侯 exists whenever there's an unfulfilled promise or expectation. It highlights the possibility of negative repercussions – like a failed bake sale, or a missed deadline. It doesn't cause the problem; it simply points out that one could occur.

Try it yourself: Consider this scenario: Your team is supposed to finish a report by Friday evening, but no one seems to be working on it. What might 侯 represent in this situation? It represents the possibility of a late report, which would negatively impact the project timeline – a potential problem that could arise if the expectation of timely completion isn’t met.
