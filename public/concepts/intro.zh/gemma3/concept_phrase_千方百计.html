🌸 Phrase 千方百计

Let's talk about a phrase that’s incredibly common in Chinese: 千方百计 (qiān fāng bǎi jì). It sounds quite complex at first, but it actually expresses a very straightforward idea – doing everything possible!

Everyday Example: Imagine your friend is trying to find a specific book in a huge library. They might say, “我用千方百计去找这本书！” (Wǒ yòng qiān fāng bǎi jì zhǎo kàn shū!). This means "I'm using every possible method to look for this book!"

Picture this: You’re trying to bake a cake for your mom’s birthday. You check the recipe, you look up tips online, you even ask your grandma for advice – all because you want to make it perfect! 千方百计 is like that feeling of putting in every effort.

Simple rule: The phrase literally translates as “thousand ways hundred methods.” "千 (qiān)" means "thousand," "方 (fāng)" means "way" or "method,” and “百 (bǎi)” means “hundred.” So, it’s all about maximizing your efforts.

Try it yourself: Think of a time you really wanted to achieve something – maybe learning a new skill, helping someone in need, or finishing a difficult project. How would you describe the effort you put in using 千方百计? You might say: “为了成功，我用千方百计努力学习！” (Wèi yào chénggōng, wǒ yòng qiān fāng bǎi jì nǔlì xuéxí!) – "To succeed, I work hard using every possible method!"
