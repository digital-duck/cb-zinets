🍃 本

Let’s imagine you're organizing your books. You might use a box to hold them all – that box is like a ‘本’. In Chinese, “本” (běn) means "book" or, more broadly, “volume.” It can also mean “origin,” “basis,” or even “self.” Think of it as the fundamental unit you’re using.

Picture this: You have a set of building blocks. Each individual block is a ‘本’. You can combine many of these '本' to build something bigger, like a tower.

Simple rule: A ‘本’ represents a single, complete thing – whether it’s a physical book, a specific piece of information, or even the starting point of an idea. It’s a basic building block for understanding and organizing things.

Try it yourself: Let's say you are learning about apples. You could create a ‘本’ (běn) – a single page – dedicated entirely to apple facts. This '本' would contain everything you know about apples on that one page, like their color, taste, and where they grow. This basic unit is “本”.
