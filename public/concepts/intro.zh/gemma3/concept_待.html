🍃 待

🍃 待

Let’s talk about a really common idea – waiting – that’s at the heart of the philosophy of shūquài dāi tù (守株待兔), which literally means “waiting for rabbits by a well.” Think about it: you're sitting near a well, hoping a rabbit will wander in and get stuck. You don’t chase after it, you don’t try to lure it – you just wait.

Everyday example: Imagine you’re waiting for a phone call from a friend. You keep checking your phone every few minutes, hoping they'll ring. You aren’t doing anything to make the call happen; you’re simply anticipating it.

Picture this: A student sits in class, passively listening to the teacher without taking notes or asking questions. They are waiting for the information to magically stick in their brain.

Simple rule: “待” (dài) means "to wait" or "to expect." It’s about relying on something happening without actively trying to make it happen. It's a state of being receptive, hoping for an event to occur based on past experience or simply because you believe it might.

Try it yourself: Let's say you see rain clouds gathering in the sky. You wait for the rain. You don’t run inside; you just observe and anticipate the precipitation. This is a simple example of "待" – you are waiting for something to occur, based on the visual evidence of the clouds. It's important to recognize that simply waiting isn’t always effective!
