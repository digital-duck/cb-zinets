🍃 易

Let’s talk about something incredibly common: ease. Think about making a cup of tea. You probably don't spend hours agonizing over the perfect brew – it's pretty straightforward, right? That sense of “easy” is what we’re going to explore today.

Everyday Example: Imagine trying to tie your shoelaces for the first time. It might feel frustrating and complicated, maybe even a little bit difficult. But after a few tries, you learn the steps, and it becomes easy.

Picture this: You're walking down a smooth, flat path compared to a rocky mountain trail. Which one feels easier? The path is ‘易’ – easy. It’s about how simple something appears or feels.

Simple rule: “易” describes something that requires little effort or difficulty. It’s the opposite of “难”(nán - difficult). It's a feeling, a perception of simplicity.

Try it yourself: Let's consider learning to ride a bicycle. Initially, it feels 难 – hard. But with practice and guidance, you learn balance, steering, and pedaling. Eventually, riding becomes 易. The key difference is the change in your experience. You’re moving from something difficult to something that feels natural and straightforward. “易” represents this shift from difficulty to simplicity.
