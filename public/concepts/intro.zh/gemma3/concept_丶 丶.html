Let’s talk about something that might feel a little confusing at first – it's called “没头没脑,” which we'll break down step-by-step. It doesn’t have a direct translation in English, but essentially means feeling like you're drifting without direction or purpose. “没头” (mò tóu) literally translates to “without a head,” and “没脑” (méi nǎo) means "without brain" – think of it as your thoughts just floating around with no clear goal.

Everyday example: Imagine you’re scrolling through social media, looking at pictures for hours. You don't really want to do anything specific, you just keep clicking and watching. You feel a little empty afterward, right? That feeling of aimlessness is often connected to this concept.

Picture this: Think about a small boat on the ocean with no rudder or sails. It’s being carried along by the waves, but it has nowhere it's trying to go. It’s just… moving.

Simple rule: 没头没脑 means you're not actively choosing where you want to go or what you want to do. You're letting things happen to you instead of taking control.

Try it yourself: Let’s say you have an afternoon free. Instead of just defaulting to watching TV, ask yourself: “What would I actually enjoy doing?” Maybe read a book, paint, or call a friend. By making a small choice, you're giving your thoughts a direction – resisting that feeling of drifting. It’s about adding a little purpose to your moments.
