Let’s think about building a really tall tower out of blocks. You start with one block, then another, and so on, right? You don't just try to put all the blocks together at once – that would be messy and probably fall down!

Picture this: you're stacking LEGO bricks. You begin by laying one brick flat on the table. That’s your first step. Then, you carefully add a second brick on top of it. Finally, you place a third brick on top of that. Each block builds upon the last – that’s how we build things!

Simple rule: To do something complicated, you need to break it down into smaller, easier steps. You tackle one step at a time. This is often called “decomposition.” It's like building with LEGOs; you don’t try to create an entire spaceship all in one go – you build the wings first, then the body, and so on.

Try it yourself: Let's say you want to learn how to bake a cake. Instead of trying to read the whole recipe at once, you might focus on just one thing: measuring out the flour. That’s one step. Then, you move onto the next – mixing the wet ingredients. Each small task is a building block in your journey to creating that delicious cake! ⺧
