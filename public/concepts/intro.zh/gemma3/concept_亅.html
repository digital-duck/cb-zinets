🌱 亅

Let’s talk about 亅 – it looks a little like a small, closed loop, doesn't it? It’s one of the basic building blocks in Chinese calligraphy and, more broadly, in East Asian character writing systems.

Everyday example: Think about drawing a circle with your finger. You start at a point, move around, and then return to that same starting point. That feeling of completing something, of returning to where you began – that’s similar to what 亅 represents.

Picture this: Imagine a little boat sailing on a lake. The boat starts at one shore, circles the entire lake, and eventually returns to its original shore. It's made a full loop!

Simple rule: 亅 is all about closure. It’s a symbol that signifies completeness, returning, or finishing a cycle. In writing, it often appears at the end of words, indicating that the thought or idea has been fully expressed.

Try it yourself: Grab a piece of paper and a pen or pencil. Draw 亅 several times. Notice how its shape is perfectly symmetrical – meaning that if you were to fold it in half along any line, both halves would be identical. This balance and symmetry are important characteristics often found within the characters that use 亅, such as 义 (yì - righteousness) or ∪ (zhe - edge). Practicing drawing 亅 will help you get a feel for its unique shape and understand why it’s such a significant symbol.
