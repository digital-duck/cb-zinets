🍃 能

🍃 能

Let’s talk about “能” (néng), which is a really important part of the idea of 熟能生巧. It's often translated as "ability" or “capacity,” but it’s actually much more than just knowing something. Think about learning to ride a bicycle. When you first get on, you wobble, you fall, and you struggle to keep your balance. You don't really know how to ride yet, right?

Picture this: Your grandpa has been riding a bike for fifty years. He can turn corners smoothly, shift gears without thinking, and even carry a passenger. He doesn’t spend time consciously calculating the angles of his turns or analyzing the physics of balance; he just does it. That's “能” in action!

The simple rule is this: "能" refers to your potential – the power within you to do something well, after repeated practice. It’s not about instantly mastering a skill, but about building up that inner capacity through effort. It's like building muscle; the more you use it, the stronger it becomes.

Try it yourself: Think of something you’d like to learn – maybe playing an instrument, speaking a new language, or even cooking a complex dish. At first, it will feel difficult and clumsy. But with consistent practice, your “能” - your ability – will grow. Don't give up! Every little bit of effort builds on itself.
