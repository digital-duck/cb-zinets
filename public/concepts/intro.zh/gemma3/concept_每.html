🍃 每 (Měi) – A Crowd’s Measure

Let's talk about how we understand large groups of people together. It’s a really common experience – think about trying to buy concert tickets, or attending a major sporting event. You might find yourself surrounded by many people, waiting in line, all wanting the same thing. This feeling of being part of a huge crowd is what we want to explore today.

Everyday Example: Imagine you're going to see your favorite K-pop band perform live. You arrive at the stadium hours before the show starts. There are thousands of other fans – people from all over the country, and maybe even the world! – waiting patiently in line for entry.

Picture this: You’re standing shoulder-to-shoulder with strangers, all sharing the same excitement and anticipation. The air is buzzing with conversation, music, and a general sense of energy. You can see people around you eagerly checking their phones, hoping to get a glimpse of the band.

Simple Rule: “每” (měi) literally means "each," but in this context, it describes how much a crowd matters. It’s not just about the number of people; it's about the density – how closely packed they are and the impact that density has on the experience. It’s a way to quantify the feeling of being “人山人海” (rén shān rén hǎi) - literally, “people mountains, people seas,” which describes an extremely crowded scene.

Try it yourself: Next time you witness a large gathering – perhaps at a festival or a protest – try to describe how dense the crowd feels. Is it comfortable? Overwhelming? Use words like "packed," "crowded," or "tight" to capture the essence of “每.”
