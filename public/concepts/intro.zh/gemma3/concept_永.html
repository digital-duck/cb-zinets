🍃 永

Let's think about something that seems to last a very long time: your favorite song. You might have heard it on the radio for years, and even though new music comes out constantly, it still feels familiar and comforting. That feeling of something enduring – of sticking around – is what we’re exploring today with the idea of “永” (yǒng).

Picture this: you build a sandcastle on the beach. It looks amazing at first, right? You carefully shape the towers and walls. But then the waves come in, and slowly, bit by bit, the castle disappears. It's gone. That’s one way to experience time – fleeting and temporary.

Now, consider a really old tree. It might be hundreds of years old! It has weathered storms, seen generations come and go, and still stands tall. “永” (yǒng) is about that kind of long-lasting quality.

Simple rule: “永” describes something that continues for a very, very long time – much longer than most things we usually encounter. Think of it like this: if something can exist or be true for an unusually extended period, it’s described with “永”. It doesn't mean forever (that’s a different concept!), but it means significantly longer than what’s typical.

Try it yourself: When you think about your grandparents, do they seem like people who have been around forever? That feeling – that sense of their history and experience – is related to the idea of “永”. The longer someone lives, the more "永" associated with them becomes.
