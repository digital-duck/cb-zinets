Simple Rule: ‘Duì niú tán qín’ means to explain something complicated in a way that the person you’re talking to doesn't understand. It’s about tailoring your communication to your audience – making sure they can actually follow along.

Try it Yourself: Let's say you're trying to teach someone how to bake bread. You could start by explaining the science behind yeast fermentation, temperature gradients, and gluten development. That would be ‘duì niú tán qín’. Instead, you might begin with a simple recipe: "Mix flour, water, and yeast. Let it rise." This is more approachable for someone new to baking.
