🌸 Phrase 狐假虎威

Think about the new kid at school who keeps saying "My older brother is on the football team" every time someone challenges him. He is not powerful himself — he is borrowing someone else's power to seem scary. That borrowed authority is the heart of this phrase.

Picture this: a small fox gets caught by a enormous tiger. Instead of panicking, the fox says, "All the animals in the forest are terrified of me — walk behind me and you will see." The tiger follows, and sure enough, every creature they pass runs away in fear. The fox looks triumphant. But the animals are not fleeing from the fox at all — they are fleeing from the tiger walking right behind it. The fox 狐假虎威: it is using the tiger's might as if it were its own.

The simple rule: 狐假虎威 describes anyone who bluffs their way to authority by leaning on a more powerful person or institution. The word 假 here means to borrow (not false), and 威 means might or prestige. Put together: fox borrows tiger's might.
