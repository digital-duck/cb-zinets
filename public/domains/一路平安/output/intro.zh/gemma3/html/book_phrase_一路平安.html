🌸 Phrase 一路平安

Let’s talk about a phrase you might hear often in China – “一路平安” (yī lù píng ān). It sounds a little complicated at first, but it’s actually incredibly simple and full of good wishes.

Everyday Example: Imagine your friend is going on a long journey, maybe driving across the country or flying to another city. You want them to have a safe trip, right?

Picture this: You're saying goodbye to someone who’s about to embark on a significant adventure – perhaps a business trip, a visit with family far away, or even just a long walk.

Simple rule: “一路平安” literally translates to "one road, safe and well.” Let’s break it down:

  • “一路” (yī lù) means “one road” or "the whole journey." It represents the entire trip.
  • “平安” (píng ān) means “peaceful,” “safe,” or “well.”

So, when you say “一路平安,” you're wishing someone a safe and trouble-free journey – for the entire length of their travels. It’s like saying "May your trip be free from danger" or “I hope everything goes smoothly.”

Try it yourself: Next time you hear someone use this phrase, try repeating it silently to yourself – “一路平安”. You'll feel a little bit of that good luck and concern being sent your way too! It’s a beautiful expression of care.
