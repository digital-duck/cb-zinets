The phrase 废寝忘食 (fèi qǐn wàng shí) is built from four characters, each carrying a clear image. 废 (fèi) means to neglect or abandon. 寝 (qǐn) means sleep. 忘 (wàng) means to forget. 食 (shí) means food. Put them together and you get: "neglect sleep, forget food." The phrase paints a portrait of someone so deeply focused on a task that the basic rhythms of daily life — eating and sleeping — simply slip away.

Simple rule. Use 废寝忘食 to describe a person working or studying with extraordinary devotion. It is a compliment, not a criticism. You might hear it said of a dedicated scientist, a student preparing for exams, or an artist lost in a new painting.

她为了完成论文，废寝忘食地工作了三天。 She worked tirelessly on her thesis, forgetting to eat and sleep for three days.

Try it yourself. Can you think of someone in your life — or a character from a story — who has ever been so passionate about something that time and meals disappeared? Try describing them using 废寝忘食 in a short sentence.
