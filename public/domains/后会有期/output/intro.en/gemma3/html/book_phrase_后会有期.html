🌸 Phrase 后会有期

Let’s talk about a really common phrase in Chinese – “后会有期” (hòu yǒu qī). It's one you’ll hear a lot, especially when someone is saying goodbye. Don't worry if it seems confusing at first; we’re going to break it down step by step.

Everyday Example: Imagine you’re meeting a friend for coffee, and as you finish your drinks, they say “后会有期!” It means they’re not just saying goodbye – they’re expressing a wish to see you again in the future.

Picture this: You're leaving a party. Your host says "后会有期," picturing a time when you might meet up again for another celebration, perhaps with more people or a different activity. It’s like saying, “I hope we can connect again sometime.”

Simple rule: “后 (hòu)” means “after” or “later”. “有 (yǒu)” means “to have”. And “期 (qī)” refers to “a period of time” or “an opportunity”. So, literally, it translates to something like "later there will be an opportunity."

Try it yourself: The next time you’re saying goodbye to someone – a classmate, a colleague, even a shopkeeper – and feel like you want to wish them well for the future, try saying “后会有期!” It's a warm and friendly way to express hope for continued contact. It’s a beautiful expression of goodwill!
