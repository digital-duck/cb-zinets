Let’s talk about a really interesting idea called “自食其言” (zìshí qítuán). It sounds complicated, but it's actually quite simple to understand.

Imagine you tell your friend you’re going to bake a cake tomorrow. You say, "I'm definitely going to make a chocolate cake!" Now, the next day, you actually do it – you bake that chocolate cake. That’s essentially what “自食其言” means. It comes from the old saying: “He who eats his own words.”

Picture this: You make a prediction about something—maybe you guess the weather will be sunny, or that your favorite team will win a game. Then, if that thing actually happens, you’ve "eaten your own words." It's like you proved your statement to be true by experiencing it yourself.

Simple rule: “自食其言” describes a situation where someone’s prediction or statement comes true. It’s about verifying your own beliefs through experience. The phrase suggests reliability and trustworthiness – if you consistently ‘eat your own words’, people will start to believe what you say!

Try it yourself: Think of something simple you can predict. Maybe you'll eat an apple later today. If you do, you’ve “自食其言”! It highlights the importance of backing up claims with reality.

Payoff 🎯

自食其言，直译过来就是“自己吃自己的言”，听起来可能有些奇怪。它其实是一种非常朴实的、强大的思考方式。我们都希望自己的想法都能实现，对吧？但如果只是空想，最终往往会失望。自食其言的核心思想是：要将你的想法转化为行动，并去验证这些想法是否真的成立。

Picture this: 你说你每天跑步可以减肥 (想象一下)。这只是一种愿望，一种希望。 简单规则就是：当你提出一个想法时，就要思考“我怎么才能证明这个想法是真的？” 自食其言不是鼓励我们去幻想美好的未来，而是提醒我们要用实际行动去检验我们的信念。

Try it yourself: 你想学习一门新的语言？ “自食其言”就意味着你不仅要报课程，还要每天学一点点，并尝试用这门语言进行简单的交流。 只有通过实践，才能知道自己是否真的能够掌握它。
