🌸 Phrase 自食其言

Let’s talk about a simple idea called “自食其言” (zìshí qítuán). It sounds complicated, but it’s really quite straightforward. Think about when you make a promise to yourself – like saying, "I'm going to eat healthier this week." Then, you actually do eat healthier!

Picture this: You tell your friend you'll finish reading a book by the end of the month. You diligently read each chapter, and you successfully complete the book before the deadline. That’s essentially what “自食其言” means.

Simply put, it means to verify your own words or beliefs. It's about proving that something you’ve said is true by actually doing it. The term literally translates to "eat your own words." Imagine saying a statement and then acting in accordance with it – like eating the words themselves!

Simple rule: If you say you will do something, try to do it. If you succeed, you've “eaten your own words,” which demonstrates that your initial belief or promise was valid.

Try it yourself: Say to yourself, "I will practice drawing for 30 minutes every day this week." Then, actually do it for the next seven days. Did you follow through? If so, you’ve successfully “食其言”!
