🌸 Phrase 东奔西走 (dōng běn xī zǒu)

Let’s explore a phrase that often describes how we feel when we have too many things on our minds: “东奔西走” (dōng běn xī zǒu). It means to rush around in different directions.

Everyday Example: Suppose you've told your family you’ll help them with several chores – cleaning the house, baking a cake, and fixing a broken appliance. You start on each task quickly, moving from one to another without finishing any properly.

Picture this: Imagine yourself jumping between these tasks like a bouncy ball, constantly changing what you're doing! It can feel quite hectic.

Simple rule: “东奔西走” describes when someone is switching between many things without really focusing on anything for long enough to complete it well. It suggests that they aren’t settled or have a clear plan. Think of it like trying to catch butterflies – you move quickly, but don't stay with one butterfly for very long.

Try it yourself: Let’s say you start learning an instrument. You buy the instrument, download tutorials, and try to practice every day without really sticking to a lesson or developing any skills. If you feel overwhelmed by this scattered effort, you might say, “我真是东奔西走!” (Wǒ zhēn shì dōng běn xī zǒu!) – “I'm really running around everywhere!” This phrase helps you recognize when your energy is spread too thin.
