Simple rule: 卉 means plants in general — not one particular flower or tree, but the whole lush family of growing things. Think of it as a collective noun for vegetation. It appears most often in literary or poetic writing, wherever the author wants to evoke a sense of nature's abundance rather than name a single species.

🌱 大

Have you ever spread your arms as wide as possible to show a friend just how big something was? Maybe you caught a fish and wanted to say, "It was this big!" That natural gesture is exactly what the character 大 captures.

Ancient Chinese scribes drew a stick figure with outstretched arms, and over thousands of years it simplified into the three-stroke shape you see today: one horizontal stroke crossing a central vertical stroke, with a left-leaning leg completing the figure.

Simple rule. 大 means big, large, or great. It is one of the most common characters in the language because size matters everywhere — big cities (大城市), big people (大人, meaning adult), big ideas. When you see 大 inside a more complex character or word, it almost always brings a sense of magnitude or importance.
