🌸 Phrase 前因后果

Let’s talk about something really fundamental to how we understand the world: 前因后果 (qián yīn hòu guǒ). It's a Chinese phrase that directly translates to “cause and effect,” but it carries a deeper meaning than just saying one thing leads to another. It emphasizes a continuous chain, a woven tapestry of events where each action or situation builds upon what came before.

Think about this: you decide to eat an apple. (Everyday example) Now picture this – you’re hungry, so you feel the urge for something sweet. That feeling caused you to reach for an apple. The apple itself was already in the fruit bowl, a consequence of someone buying it at the store last week. The store owner had bought it from a farmer who grew it! (Picture this)

Essentially, 前因后果 is about recognizing that nothing happens in isolation. There’s always a ‘before’ and an ‘after,’ and those ‘before’ events played a part in creating the ‘after.’ A simple rule to remember: every effect has one or more causes. It doesn't matter how many steps there are, it’s all connected. (Simple Rule)

Let’s try this: You woke up late today. (Try it yourself) What might have caused you to wake up late? Maybe you didn't set your alarm correctly? Perhaps you had a bad dream and woke up suddenly? Each of those – the faulty alarm, the unsettling dream – was a ‘前因’ (cause) that led to the ‘后果’ (effect) of waking up late. Notice how one thing leads to another, forming a chain.
