功

Payoff 🎯

Everyday example. Think about the last time you finished a project you were proud of — maybe you cooked a meal from scratch, fixed something broken, or passed a tough exam. That feeling of it worked is exactly what 功 (gōng) captures: the achievement that comes from effort meeting skill.

Picture this. You have already met 力 — raw strength, the muscle behind any action. Now 功 adds a crucial layer: it is strength that lands, effort that succeeds. Imagine 力 as swinging a hammer; 功 is the nail driven flush. Without direction and follow-through, force alone accomplishes nothing. 功 is the proof that your energy was well spent.

Simple rule. 功 = effort + result. It appears whenever Chinese talks about merit, achievement, or success — from 功课 (homework, literally "achievement lessons") to 功夫 (kung fu, "mastery through time and effort"). It is the natural endpoint of our journey because every concept before it — structure, motion, force — was building toward this moment: something accomplished.

Try it yourself. Now read the phrase 马到成功 (mǎ dào chéng gōng) — "success the moment the horse arrives." It paints a picture of victory so swift it feels effortless, like a cavalry charge that wins on arrival. Each character you have learned clicks into place: the horse (马) arrives (到), completion forms (成), and achievement is sealed (功).
