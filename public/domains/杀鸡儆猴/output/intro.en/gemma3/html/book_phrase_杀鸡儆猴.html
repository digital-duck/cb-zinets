The simple rule here is this: 乛 一 (pronounced "chui yi") represents a deliberate action taken to demonstrate the seriousness of a situation. It’s about making a visible consequence for a specific, negative behavior. Think of it like a signal – a clear message sent to everyone involved.

It's not necessarily about punishment; it’s about highlighting the potential impact of continuing that behavior. The action itself—the "chui yi"—is designed to grab attention and drive home the point. Picture this: A farmer has chickens and ducks in his yard. He notices one duck is repeatedly escaping its pen. To discourage other ducks from doing the same, he catches the troublesome duck and severely punishes it. The remaining ducks quickly learn to stay within their pens.

Simple rule: “杀鸡儆猴” (shī jī qìng huò) literally translates to "kill a chicken to frighten monkeys.” It’s an idiom used to describe a situation where a single person is punished severely, not necessarily for being the worst offender, but to discourage others from misbehaving. The goal isn't just about punishing one individual; it’s about sending a strong message to everyone else.

Try it yourself: Think of a time you’ve seen someone use this strategy – perhaps in a school setting or even in everyday life. It's about using a visible consequence to change behavior across a group. The key is the public nature of the correction and its intended effect on others.
