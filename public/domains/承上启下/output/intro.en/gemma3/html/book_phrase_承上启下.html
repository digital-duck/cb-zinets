Picture this: you're writing a story. The first chapter sets the scene – that’s your “承上” (carry what is previous). The simple rule is: every idea or step should be connected to what came before and provide a foundation for what comes after. Try it yourself: Imagine you’re explaining how to bake a cake. You start with gathering ingredients (“承上”) – flour, sugar, eggs.
