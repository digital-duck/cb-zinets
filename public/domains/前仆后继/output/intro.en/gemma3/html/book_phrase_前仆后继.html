🌸 Phrase 前仆后继

Let’s talk about a really powerful idea from Chinese history and culture – the phrase “前仆后继” (qián pǔ hòu jì). It sounds complicated, but it's actually quite simple to understand.

Everyday Example: Imagine your soccer team is losing badly in the final minutes of a game. Instead of giving up, players keep running forward, trying to score, even though they’re likely to be tackled or stopped. They’re not stopping; they're still pushing forward.

Picture this: Think about soldiers marching into battle. They might face overwhelming odds – many more enemies than themselves. But they don't retreat. Instead, they keep moving forward, stepping over the bodies of fallen comrades. This is the essence of “前仆后继.”

Simple rule: It means "to step before and then step after," or, even simpler, “to follow those who have gone ahead, bravely facing danger or difficulty." It’s about perseverance – continuing to push forward despite setbacks. The order is important: before (前 - qián) implies leading the way, and after (后 - hòu) signifies following with courage.

Try it yourself: Think of a time you faced a challenge. Maybe you were learning a new skill that was hard at first. Did you give up when things got tough? Or did you keep practicing, inspired by those who came before you and succeeded? That spirit – that willingness to step forward even when it’s difficult – embodies “前仆后继.”
