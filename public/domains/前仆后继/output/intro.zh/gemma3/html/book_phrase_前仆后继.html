🌸 Phrase 前仆后继

Let’s talk about a powerful Chinese phrase: “前仆后继” (qián pǔ hòu jì). It sounds a bit complex at first, but it's actually quite straightforward. Let's break it down step by step.

Everyday Example: Imagine you’re playing tag with your friends. If someone is being chased and falls down, the others don't just stand there laughing; they keep running to help them get back in the game. That’s a small example of this idea!

Picture This: Now picture a line of soldiers marching forward into battle. They might face danger, but they continue moving ahead, one after another, without stopping or turning back.

Simple Rule: “前” (qián) means “front,” “仆” (pǔ) means “fall” or “kneel,” and “后” (hòu) means “behind” or “after.” “继” (jì) means "continue" or "follow." So, literally translated, it’s like saying "falling forward, followed by others.” It describes a situation where people are willing to face danger or difficulty, and those who have already faced it encourage others to do the same.

Try it yourself: Think about a time you saw someone bravely stand up for what they believed in, even when it was difficult. Or perhaps a group of volunteers working tirelessly to help others – that’s “前仆后继” in action! It's about courage and support, working together towards a common goal.
