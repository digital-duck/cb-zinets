🌸 Phrase 火上加油

Imagine two friends arguing loudly in a room. The tension is already high — voices raised, faces flushed. Now a third person walks in and says, "Well, you started it!" The argument explodes into something far worse.

That moment — when someone makes a bad situation even worse — is exactly what 火上加油 (huǒ shàng jiā yóu) captures.

Picture this: A small campfire is burning. Instead of letting it die down, someone pours oil onto it. The flames leap up, the fire grows dangerously large. The oil didn't solve anything; it made everything more intense.

Breaking down the characters:

  • 火 (huǒ) means fire
  • 上 (shàng) means on top of or onto
  • 加 (jiā) means to add
  • 油 (yóu) means oil

Literally: "add oil onto fire." Together, the phrase means to make an already tense or difficult situation even worse — like pouring fuel on a flame.

Simple rule: Whenever someone's actions escalate a conflict, a crisis, or someone's anger instead of calming it down, you can say they are 火上加油.
