🌸 Phrase 以一当十

Everyday example. Imagine your school is short-staffed for a bake sale, so one super-organized parent steps up and handles decorating, pricing, cashiering, and cleanup — doing the work of a whole team all by herself. At the end of the day everyone laughs and says, "She's worth ten people!"

Picture this. In ancient China, a single fearless soldier could hold a narrow mountain pass against a much larger army. Because of his skill and courage, that one defender was as effective as an entire squad. The image stuck: one person standing in for ten.

Simple rule. The idiom 以一当十 (yǐ yī dāng shí) is built from four characters:

Character Sound Meaning
以 yǐ using / with
一 yī one
当 dāng to equal / to stand for
十 shí ten

Put together, the phrase says: using one to equal ten. It praises someone whose effort, skill, or courage makes them worth far more than just one person. In math-like shorthand: $1 \times \text{effort} = 10 \times \text{result}$.

Try it yourself. Think of someone in your life — a teacher, a friend, a family member — who always seems to accomplish twice (or ten times!) as much as anyone else. Can you write one sentence in Chinese or English describing them using the spirit of 以一当十?

Payoff 🎯

Imagine you are moving apartments, and instead of carrying one box at a time, you find a dolly that lets you stack ten boxes and wheel them all in a single trip. That dolly is the spirit of 以一当十 (yǐ yī dāng shí) — literally "use one to do the work of ten."

Picture this. A skilled chef's knife, kept perfectly sharp, glides through vegetables in moments that would take a dull blade ten times as long. The knife did not change; the quality of its edge multiplied its effect. That is exactly what this phrase captures: one well-placed, well-prepared action carrying the weight of many.

The simple rule. When you concentrate effort, skill, or the right tool at the right moment, a single action can achieve what ten scattered actions cannot. The phrase is not about shortcuts — it is about leverage: understanding a situation so well that your one move is worth ten of anyone else's.

Try it yourself. Think of one area in your life — studying, cooking, or a hobby — where you often repeat the same small effort many times with little result. Ask yourself: is there one deeper preparation (a better study method, a sharper skill, a clearer plan) that would make each attempt count for ten?
