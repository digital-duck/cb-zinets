🌸 Phrase 鸡犬不宁

Everyday example. Imagine your neighbors decide to throw a massive party — music blasting, people shouting, cars honking all night. Even your dog hides under the bed and your cat refuses to eat. Nothing feels settled. That restless, chaotic feeling is exactly what this phrase captures.

Picture this. 鸡犬不宁 (jī quǎn bù níng) is made of four characters: 鸡 means chicken, 犬 means dog, 不 means not, and 宁 means peaceful or calm. Put them together and you get: "even the chickens and dogs are not at peace." In traditional Chinese villages, chickens and dogs were the most ordinary, ever-present animals. If even they were disturbed, the situation must have been truly chaotic.

Simple rule. Use 鸡犬不宁 to describe a scene of total disruption — not just mild inconvenience, but full-on disorder that affects everyone and everything around it. It often describes noise, conflict, or upheaval that spreads through a household or community.
