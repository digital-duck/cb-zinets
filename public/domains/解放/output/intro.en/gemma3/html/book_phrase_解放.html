Simple rule: In Chinese, “万” (wàn) is often used as a prefix to mean “ten thousand.” So, "一万" (yī wàn) means ten thousand, and “二万” (èr wàn) means twenty thousand. It’s like saying ‘lots of’ or ‘a great number of’.

Try it yourself: Let's say you have 500 coins. You could say you have "五千" (wǔ qiān), which is five thousand – just by adding the “万” prefix! It’s a quick and easy way to express a very large quantity.
