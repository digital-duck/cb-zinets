##勹

Let’s talk about a really interesting idea called “勹”, which is a simple but powerful tool for understanding how things change. It's often described as "looping back" or “feedback,” but let’s break it down in a way that makes sense.

Everyday Example: Think about saving money. You get paid, you put some of it into savings, and then you spend some of the money you saved last month. You’re constantly moving money between two places – your paycheck and your savings account.

Picture this: Imagine a hamster running on a wheel. The hamster runs, the wheel turns, and the hamster keeps running because of the turning wheel. It's a cycle!

Simple rule: “勹” describes any situation where something happening now affects what happens next, and that new thing then influences what happens again later. Essentially, it’s about things influencing each other in a repeating pattern.

Try it yourself: Let's look at another example: a thermostat in your house. The temperature drops below the set point (say, 20°C), so the heater turns on. The heater warms the room up until it reaches 20°C, and then the heater turns off. Then the room starts to cool down again... back to the beginning! This is a basic “勹” - a cycle of temperature change triggered by a thermostat. You can see how the thermostat's action creates a loop that repeats over and over.
