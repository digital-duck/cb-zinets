Simple rule: “丿一” (yī) means “one less.” It describes a process where something decreases by exactly one unit each step. It’s not about big jumps; it's about small, consistent reductions. Mathematically, we can represent this as: (x_n = x_{n-1} - 1), where (x_n) is the value at step n, and (x_{n-1}) is the value at the previous step.
