🌸 语录 丰衣足食

Let’s say you’re a baker. You spend your days making delicious bread and cakes. At the end of each week, you have plenty of money – you have “丰衣” (fēng yī) – which means having enough wealth or income. And because you sell so many treats, you always have food in your house – you have “足食” (zú shí) – meaning you never go hungry and always have enough to eat.

Picture this: a farmer who grows rice. He harvests a huge amount of grain. Because he has so much grain, he can sell it for profit, giving him lots of money. And because he has so much grain, his family doesn’t worry about running out of food – they always have enough to eat.

The simple rule is this: 丰衣足食 (fēng yī zú shí) describes a situation where someone has both wealth and sufficient food. It's a balanced state of having enough money and enough resources to meet basic needs, like food. It doesn’t just mean being rich; it means being secure in all aspects of life.

Try it yourself: Think about your own life. Do you have enough money for the things you want? And do you always have enough food to eat? If so, you could say you live a little like 丰衣足食! It’s a nice way to describe feeling comfortable and secure in your circumstances.
