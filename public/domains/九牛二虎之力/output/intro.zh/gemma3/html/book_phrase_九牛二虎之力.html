🌸 Phrase 九牛二虎之力 (jiǔ niú èr hǔ zhī lì)

Let’s explore a common Chinese idiom, “九牛二虎之力” (jiǔ niú èr hǔ zhī lì). It might sound tricky at first, but we'll explain it simply. This phrase is used to describe something that demands a huge amount of effort – really hard!

Everyday Example: Suppose you’re trying to move a large piece of furniture by yourself. You push and strain, feeling completely exhausted. Someone might say, “这需要九牛二虎之力！” (Zhè xūyào jiǔ niú èr hǔ zhī lì!) – "This needs nine oxen and two tigers!"

Picture this: Imagine nine strong oxen pulling together with all their strength. That's a lot of power! Then picture two fierce tigers joining them, adding even more force. The idiom uses this image to show you how much effort is needed to accomplish something difficult.

Simple rule: “九牛二虎之力” literally means "nine oxen and two tigers," but it’s an exaggeration – a way of saying something is extremely hard. It doesn't mean you actually need those animals! It just highlights the immense effort required.
