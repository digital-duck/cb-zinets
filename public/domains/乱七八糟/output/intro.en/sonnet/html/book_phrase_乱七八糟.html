Everyday example. Imagine you've just come home after a long day, and your younger sibling has been "helping" tidy your room. Simple rule. Use 乱七八糟 whenever something — a room, a plan, a situation — is thoroughly disorganized. It works as an adjective placed after the thing being described: 房间乱七八糟 (fángjiān luàn qī bā zāo) means "the room is a total mess."
