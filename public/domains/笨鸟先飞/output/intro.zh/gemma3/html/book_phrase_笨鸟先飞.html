🌸 Phrase 笨鸟先飞

Let’s imagine you're learning to ride a bike. At first, it feels really hard, right? You wobble, you fall, and maybe even get a little frustrated. But with practice, slowly but surely, you start to balance better. You might see someone who seems to pick it up instantly – let’s call them “the fast learner.” They seem effortless, while you’re still struggling.

Picture this: you're trying to build something complex, like a Lego castle. Some people immediately start putting on the tallest towers and most intricate details. Others begin with a simple baseplate, building it level by level, making sure each piece fits correctly before adding another.

The simple rule is this: 笨鸟先飞 (bèn niǎo xiān fēi). This phrase literally translates to “the clumsy bird flies first.” It means that it’s okay – and often better – to start with the basics, even if they seem difficult or slow. Don't try to jump ahead and do everything at once. Focus on mastering the foundational steps before moving on to more complicated tasks.

Try it yourself: If you’re learning a new programming language, don't immediately attempt to write complex applications. Start with simple “Hello, world!” programs. Build your knowledge step-by-step, just like that clumsy bird learning to fly. The key is consistent progress through the basics.
