When we say someone is dǎn xiǎo rú shǔ, we are not just saying they are slightly nervous; we mean their fear makes them almost freeze up.

The phrase uses the image of the mouse to show that the level of timidity is maximal and instinctive. The simple rule for using this idiom is: Reserve it only for describing extreme cases of cowardice or crippling nervousness.
